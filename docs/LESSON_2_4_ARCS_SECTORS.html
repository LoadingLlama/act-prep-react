Understanding arcs and sectors is crucial for ACT circle geometry questions. These concepts appear in 5-8 questions per test and build directly on your knowledge of circles and angles. Mastering this topic will help you tackle some of the test's most elegant geometry problems!

1. Central Angles and Arcs

A central angle is an angle whose vertex sits at the center of a circle. An arc is the curved portion of the circle's edge (circumference) between two points.

The Key Relationship

The measure of an arc (in degrees) equals the measure of its central angle. If the central angle is 60°, the arc is also 60°.

  • A full circle = 360°
  • A semicircle (half circle) = 180°
  • A quarter circle = 90°

In a circle, a central angle measures 45°. What is the measure of its corresponding arc?

A. 22.5°
B. 45°
C. 90°
D. 135°
E. 180°

Solution:

  • The arc measure equals the central angle measure
  • Central angle = 45°, so arc = 45°
  • Remember: Arc measure is NOT the same as arc length (which we'll learn next)

Answer: B

2. Arc Length

Arc length is the actual distance along the curved line of the arc—like measuring the arc with a string. This is different from arc measure (which is just the angle).

Formula: Arc Length = (θ/360°) × 2πr

Breaking Down the Formula

  • θ = central angle in degrees
  • 2πr = circumference of the entire circle
  • (θ/360°) = what fraction of the circle you're measuring
  • Key insight: You're finding a fraction of the total circumference!

ACT Strategy

Think of it this way: if the central angle is 90° (one quarter of 360°), then the arc length is 1/4 of the circumference. If the angle is 180° (half of 360°), the arc length is 1/2 of the circumference.

A circle has radius 6. What is the length of an arc with a central angle of 60°?

A. π
B. 2π
C. 3π
D. 6π
E. 12π

Solution:

  • Arc Length = (θ/360°) × 2πr
  • Arc Length = (60°/360°) × 2π(6)
  • Simplify the fraction: 60/360 = 1/6
  • Arc Length = (1/6) × 12π = 2π
  • Check: 60° is 1/6 of 360°, so arc is 1/6 of circumference ✓

Answer: B

3. Sector Area

A sector is a "slice" of a circle, like a piece of pizza. It's the region bounded by two radii and an arc. The sector area measures the space inside this slice.

Formula: Sector Area = (θ/360°) × πr²

The Pattern

Notice the beautiful pattern:

  • Arc length uses the fraction × circumference (2πr)
  • Sector area uses the fraction × circle area (πr²)
  • Both use the same fraction: (θ/360°)
  • Why? They're both finding a fraction of the whole circle!

A circle has radius 8. What is the area of a sector with a central angle of 45°?

A. 4π
B. 8π
C. 16π
D. 32π
E. 64π

Solution:

  • Sector Area = (θ/360°) × πr²
  • Sector Area = (45°/360°) × π(8)²
  • Simplify: 45/360 = 1/8
  • Sector Area = (1/8) × 64π = 8π
  • Makes sense: 45° is 1/8 of a full circle, so area is 1/8 of total!

Answer: B

4. Working Backwards from Arc Length or Area

Sometimes the ACT gives you the arc length or sector area and asks you to find the angle or radius. Don't panic—just use the same formulas, but solve for the unknown variable!

Problem-Solving Strategy

  • Write down the formula
  • Substitute the values you know
  • Solve for the unknown
  • Check if your answer makes sense (e.g., angle should be less than 360°)

A circle has radius 10 and an arc length of 5π. What is the measure of the central angle in degrees?

A. 45°
B. 60°
C. 72°
D. 90°
E. 120°

Solution:

  • Arc Length = (θ/360°) × 2πr
  • 5π = (θ/360°) × 2π(10)
  • 5π = (θ/360°) × 20π
  • Divide both sides by π: 5 = (θ/360°) × 20
  • Divide by 20: 5/20 = θ/360° → 1/4 = θ/360°
  • Solve: θ = 360° × (1/4) = 90°

Answer: D

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Arc measure equals the central angle measure (both in degrees)
  • ✓Arc length = (θ/360°) × 2πr—a fraction of the circumference
  • ✓Sector area = (θ/360°) × πr²—a fraction of the circle's area
  • ✓Both formulas use the same fraction (θ/360°) of the full circle
  • ✓Think of sectors as "pizza slices" and arcs as the "crust"
