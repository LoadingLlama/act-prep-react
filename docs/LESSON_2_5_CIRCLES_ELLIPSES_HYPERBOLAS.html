Conic sections—circles, ellipses, parabolas, and hyperbolas—represent one of the most tested coordinate geometry topics on the ACT. While these curves appear when you slice a cone at different angles, you don't need to memorize complex derivations. Instead, focus on instantly recognizing equation patterns and extracting key information like centers and radii. This lesson will help you identify conic sections in seconds and solve problems efficiently.

1. Circle Equations in Standard Form

The standard form of a circle equation immediately reveals the circle's center and radius:

(x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²

  • (h, k) is the center of the circle
  • r is the radius (not r²!)

Watch Out for Sign Changes!

The trickiest part: (x - 3) means the x-coordinate is +3, not -3. Similarly, (y + 2) = (y - (-2)) means the y-coordinate is -2. The ACT loves testing this!

ACT Strategy Tip

Always rewrite additions as subtractions: (x + 4) becomes (x - (-4)). This prevents sign errors that cost points. Also remember: if you see r² = 36, then r = 6, not 36.

What is the center and radius of the circle (x + 5)² + (y - 3)² = 49?

A. Center: (5, -3); Radius: 49
B. Center: (-5, 3); Radius: 7
C. Center: (5, 3); Radius: 7
D. Center: (-5, 3); Radius: 49
E. Center: (-5, -3); Radius: 7

Solution:

  • Rewrite (x + 5) as (x - (-5)) → h = -5
  • (y - 3) gives k = 3
  • r² = 49, so r = √49 = 7
  • Center: (-5, 3), Radius: 7

Answer: B

2. Converting General Form to Standard Form

Sometimes circles appear in general form: x² + y² + Dx + Ey + F = 0. To find the center and radius, you need to complete the square.

Completing the Square Process

  • Group x terms and y terms separately
  • For x² + Dx: take (D/2)², add and subtract it
  • For y² + Ey: take (E/2)², add and subtract it
  • Rearrange into (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r² form

Why This Works

Think about (x - 3)² = x² - 6x + 9. Notice the middle term (-6x) and constant (9). The constant is always (middle coefficient ÷ 2)². That's why we take half the coefficient and square it!

The equation x² + y² - 6x + 8y + 9 = 0 represents a circle. What is the center?

A. (3, -4)
B. (-3, 4)
C. (6, -8)
D. (-6, 8)
E. (3, 4)

Solution:

  • Group: (x² - 6x) + (y² + 8y) + 9 = 0
  • For x: (-6/2)² = 9, so (x² - 6x + 9) = (x - 3)²
  • For y: (8/2)² = 16, so (y² + 8y + 16) = (y + 4)²
  • Equation becomes: (x - 3)² - 9 + (y + 4)² - 16 + 9 = 0
  • Simplify: (x - 3)² + (y + 4)² = 16
  • Center: (3, -4) because (y + 4) = (y - (-4))

Answer: A

3. Ellipses and Hyperbolas

While circles have equal x² and y² coefficients, ellipses and hyperbolas don't. The key difference: ellipses use addition (+), hyperbolas use subtraction (-).

Ellipse Standard Form

(x - h)²/a² + (y - k)²/b² = 1

Center: (h, k). Think of an ellipse as a "squashed circle"—it's stretched differently along the x and y axes depending on whether a > b or b > a.

Hyperbola Standard Form

(x - h)²/a² - (y - k)²/b² = 1

Center: (h, k). The minus sign is the giveaway! Hyperbolas have two separate curved branches that open away from each other.

ACT Strategy: Quick Recognition

  • Same signs + equal denominators = Circle
  • Same signs + different denominators = Ellipse
  • Opposite signs (one + and one -) = Hyperbola
  • Only one squared variable = Parabola

The equation (x - 2)²/16 + (y + 1)²/9 = 1 represents which of the following?

A. A circle with center (2, -1)
B. An ellipse with center (2, -1)
C. A hyperbola with center (2, -1)
D. An ellipse with center (-2, 1)
E. A parabola with vertex (2, -1)

Solution:

  • Both terms use addition (+) → not a hyperbola
  • Different denominators (16 ≠ 9) → not a circle
  • Both variables squared → not a parabola
  • This must be an ellipse!
  • Center: (2, -1) from (x - 2) and (y + 1) = (y - (-1))

Answer: B

4. The 5-Second Identification Method

The ACT frequently asks you to identify which conic section an equation represents. Master this quick three-step process:

Step 1: Count Squared Terms

If only x² OR only y² appears → Parabola. If both x² AND y² appear → continue to Step 2.

Step 2: Check Signs

If one is positive and one is negative → Hyperbola. If both are the same sign → continue to Step 3.

Step 3: Compare Coefficients

If the coefficients (or denominators) are equal → Circle. If they're different → Ellipse.

Which of the following equations represents a hyperbola?

A. x² + y² = 25
B. x²/4 + y²/9 = 1
C. y = x² + 3x - 4
D. x²/16 - y²/4 = 1
E. (x - 3)² + (y + 2)² = 7

Solution:

  • A: Both squared, same signs, equal coefficients → Circle
  • B: Both squared, same signs, different denominators → Ellipse
  • C: Only x squared → Parabola
  • D: Both squared, opposite signs (+ and -) → Hyperbola ✓
  • E: Both squared, same signs, equal coefficients → Circle

Answer: D

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Circle standard form (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r² gives center (h, k) and radius r
  • ✓Watch for sign flips: (x + 4) = (x - (-4)), so the x-coordinate is -4
  • ✓To complete the square: take half the coefficient, square it, then balance the equation
  • ✓Quick ID: opposite signs = hyperbola, same signs with different denominators = ellipse
  • ✓Equal coefficients = circle, different coefficients = ellipse, minus sign = hyperbola
