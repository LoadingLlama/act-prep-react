Unit conversion questions appear in 2-3 problems per ACT test, testing your ability to convert between US customary units (feet, pounds, gallons) and metric units (meters, kilograms, liters). While these might seem like simple arithmetic, the ACT often creates multi-step conversions or combines units (like miles per hour to feet per second) to test your understanding of dimensional analysis. Mastering the systematic approach to unit conversion and memorizing key conversion factors ensures you won't waste time or make calculation errors on these straightforward points.

1. Dimensional Analysis Method

Dimensional analysis uses conversion factors written as fractions to cancel units systematically. This organized approach prevents errors and works for any conversion, simple or complex.

Setting Up Conversion Factors

A conversion factor equals 1 because numerator and denominator represent the same quantity. For example, 12 inches = 1 foot, so both 12 in/1 ft and 1 ft/12 in equal 1. Multiply by the form that cancels unwanted units. To convert feet to inches, multiply by (12 in/1 ft) so feet cancel: 5 ft × (12 in/1 ft) = 60 in.

Multi-Step Conversions

For complex conversions, chain multiple factors together. To convert 3 miles to inches: 3 mi × (5,280 ft/1 mi) × (12 in/1 ft) = 3 × 5,280 × 12 = 190,080 inches. Units cancel diagonally: miles cancel with miles, feet cancel with feet, leaving inches.

Area and Volume Conversions

For area conversions, square the linear conversion factor. Since 1 ft = 12 in, then 1 ft² = (12 in)² = 144 in². For volume conversions, cube it: 1 ft³ = (12 in)³ = 1,728 in³.

Example 1: Multi-Step Conversion

Convert 2.5 hours to seconds.

A) 150 seconds

B) 2,500 seconds

C) 9,000 seconds

D) 15,000 seconds

E) 90,000 seconds

Solution:

Step 1: Convert hours to minutes: 2.5 hr × (60 min/1 hr) = 150 min.

Step 2: Convert minutes to seconds: 150 min × (60 sec/1 min) = 9,000 sec.

Step 3: Or in one step: 2.5 hr × (60 min/1 hr) × (60 sec/1 min) = 2.5 × 3,600 = 9,000 sec.

Answer: C — 2.5 hours = 9,000 seconds.

2. Common US Customary Conversions

Memorizing common conversion factors within the US Customary system saves time. These appear frequently on the ACT in various contexts.

Length Conversions

Key length conversions: 1 foot = 12 inches. 1 yard = 3 feet = 36 inches. 1 mile = 5,280 feet = 1,760 yards. Remember that 1 mile ≈ 1.6 kilometers (rough approximation).

Time, Weight, and Volume

Time: 1 minute = 60 seconds, 1 hour = 60 minutes = 3,600 seconds, 1 day = 24 hours. Weight: 1 pound = 16 ounces, 1 ton = 2,000 pounds. Volume (liquid): 1 cup = 8 fluid ounces, 1 pint = 2 cups, 1 quart = 2 pints, 1 gallon = 4 quarts.

Speed Conversions

Common speed unit: miles per hour (mph). To convert mph to feet per second: multiply by (5,280 ft/1 mi) × (1 hr/3,600 sec) = 5,280/3,600 ≈ 1.47. So 60 mph ≈ 88 ft/sec.

Example 2: Volume Conversion

How many pints are in 3 gallons?

A) 6 pints

B) 12 pints

C) 18 pints

D) 24 pints

E) 48 pints

Solution:

Step 1: Convert gallons to quarts: 3 gal × (4 qt/1 gal) = 12 qt.

Step 2: Convert quarts to pints: 12 qt × (2 pt/1 qt) = 24 pt.

Answer: D — 3 gallons = 24 pints.

3. Metric System Conversions

The metric system uses powers of 10, making conversions simpler. Understanding prefixes and their meanings is key to quick metric conversions.

Metric Prefixes

Common metric prefixes: kilo- (k) = 1,000 or 10³. hecto- (h) = 100 or 10². deca- (da) = 10 or 10¹. Base unit (meter, liter, gram). deci- (d) = 0.1 or 10⁻¹. centi- (c) = 0.01 or 10⁻². milli- (m) = 0.001 or 10⁻³.

Converting Within Metric

To convert between metric units, count the steps between prefixes and move the decimal point. For example, to convert 5 kilometers to meters, move 3 places right (kilo to base = 10³): 5 km = 5,000 m. To convert 250 centimeters to meters, move 2 places left (centi to base = 10⁻²): 250 cm = 2.5 m.

Metric-US Conversions

Approximate conversions: 1 inch ≈ 2.54 centimeters (exact). 1 meter ≈ 3.28 feet ≈ 39.37 inches. 1 kilometer ≈ 0.621 miles. 1 kilogram ≈ 2.2 pounds. 1 liter ≈ 1.06 quarts ≈ 0.264 gallons.

Example 3: Metric Conversion

Convert 3,500 milliliters to liters.

A) 0.35 L

B) 3.5 L

C) 35 L

D) 350 L

E) 3,500,000 L

Solution:

Step 1: Milli- means 0.001, so 1 mL = 0.001 L, or 1,000 mL = 1 L.

Step 2: Convert: 3,500 mL × (1 L/1,000 mL) = 3.5 L.

Step 3: Or move decimal 3 places left: 3,500. → 3.500 = 3.5 L.

Answer: B — 3,500 milliliters = 3.5 liters.

4. Rate and Density Conversions

Rate conversions involve compound units like miles per hour or dollars per pound. Density conversions require understanding mass per volume relationships.

Converting Rates

To convert a rate, convert both the numerator and denominator separately. For example, convert 30 feet/minute to inches/second: Numerator: 30 ft × 12 in/ft = 360 in. Denominator: 1 min × 60 sec/min = 60 sec. Result: 360 in/60 sec = 6 in/sec.

Price Per Unit Conversions

Unit price problems ask you to find cost per unit to compare values. If 3 pounds cost $12, the unit price is $12/3 lb = $4/lb. To compare two options, convert both to the same units first, then divide to find which has lower cost per unit.

Density Conversions

Density = mass/volume. Common units: g/cm³ or kg/L. If density of water is 1 g/mL and you have 500 mL, the mass is 500 mL × 1 g/mL = 500 g. Rearrange for volume: volume = mass/density. For mass: mass = density × volume.

Example 4: Rate Conversion

A car travels at 60 miles per hour. What is this speed in feet per second? (1 mile = 5,280 feet)

A) 44 ft/sec

B) 66 ft/sec

C) 88 ft/sec

D) 120 ft/sec

E) 316,800 ft/sec

Solution:

Step 1: Set up: 60 mi/hr × (5,280 ft/1 mi) × (1 hr/3,600 sec).

Step 2: Cancel units and calculate: 60 × 5,280 / 3,600 = 316,800 / 3,600 = 88 ft/sec.

Answer: C — 60 mph = 88 feet per second.
