Mastery Check Quiz: Lines

Question 1 (Easy)

What is the slope of the line passing through (1, 3) and (5, 11)?

A. 1

B. 2

C. 3

D. 4

E. 8

Show Solution

Correct Answer: B

m = (y₂ - y₁)/(x₂ - x₁) = (11 - 3)/(5 - 1) = 8/4 = 2

Question 4 (Medium)

What is the slope of the line 3x + 6y = 18?

A. -2

B. -½

C. ½

D. 2

E. 3

Show Solution

Correct Answer: B

Convert to slope-intercept form:

3x + 6y = 18 → 6y = -3x + 18 → y = -½x + 3

The slope is -½

Question 5 (Medium)

What is the distance between points (1, 2) and (4, 6)?

A. 3

B. 4

C. 5

D. 7

E. 10

Show Solution

Correct Answer: C

d = √[(4-1)² + (6-2)²] = √[3² + 4²] = √[9 + 16] = √25 = 5

This is a 3-4-5 Pythagorean triple!

Question 7 (Medium)

Write the equation of a line with slope 2 that passes through (3, 5).

A. y = 2x + 1

B. y = 2x - 1

C. y = 2x + 5

D. y = 2x - 5

E. y = 2x + 11

Show Solution

Correct Answer: B

Use y = mx + b with m = 2 and point (3, 5):

5 = 2(3) + b → 5 = 6 + b → b = -1

So y = 2x - 1

Question 8 (Hard)

What is the equation of a line parallel to 2x + 3y = 12 that passes through the origin?

A. y = -⅔x

B. y = ⅔x

C. y = -³⁄₂x

D. y = ³⁄₂x

E. y = 2x

Show Solution

Correct Answer: A

First find slope of original line: 2x + 3y = 12 → 3y = -2x + 12 → y = -⅔x + 4

Parallel line has same slope: m = -⅔

Since it passes through origin (0,0), b = 0

So y = -⅔x

Question 9 (Hard)

Question 10 (Hard)

Line L passes through (4, -2) and is perpendicular to the line y = -⅔x + 5. What is the equation of line L?

A. y = ³⁄₂x - 8

B. y = ³⁄₂x + 4

C. y = -⅔x + 2

D. y = ³⁄₂x - 10

E. y = -³⁄₂x + 4

Show Solution

Correct Answer: A

Original slope = -⅔

Perpendicular slope = ³⁄₂ (negative reciprocal)

Using y = mx + b with m = ³⁄₂ and point (4, -2):

-2 = ³⁄₂(4) + b → -2 = 6 + b → b = -8

So y = ³⁄₂x - 8
