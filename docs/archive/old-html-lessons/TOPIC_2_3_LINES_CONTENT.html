Concept 1: Slope of a Line

Slope measures the steepness and direction of a line. It's one of the most fundamental concepts in coordinate geometry and appears frequently on the ACT.

Slope Formula

m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)

Where (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) are two points on the line

Remember: "Rise over run" = change in y / change in x

Example 1: Finding Slope from Two Points

Problem: What is the slope of the line passing through points (2, 3) and (6, 11)?

Solution:

Using m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)

m = (11 - 3) / (6 - 2) = 8/4 = 2

Answer: Slope = 2

This means for every 1 unit we move right, we move 2 units up.

Types of Slopes

  • Positive slope: Line rises from left to right (m > 0)
  • Negative slope: Line falls from left to right (m < 0)
  • Zero slope: Horizontal line (m = 0)
  • Undefined slope: Vertical line (division by zero)

Example 2: Horizontal and Vertical Lines

Problem: What is the slope of the line passing through (3, 5) and (7, 5)?

Solution:

m = (5 - 5) / (7 - 3) = 0/4 = 0

Answer: Slope = 0 (horizontal line)

Notice both points have the same y-coordinate. This always indicates a horizontal line!

Concept 2: Equations of Lines

Lines can be expressed in several different forms. Each form is useful in different contexts.

Forms of Linear Equations

  • Slope-intercept form: y = mx + b (m = slope, b = y-intercept)
  • Point-slope form: y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)
  • Standard form: Ax + By = C (A, B, C are integers)

Example 3: Writing Equation in Slope-Intercept Form

Problem: Write the equation of a line with slope 3 that passes through the point (2, 5).

Solution:

Step 1: Start with y = mx + b, where m = 3

y = 3x + b

Step 2: Substitute the point (2, 5) to find b

5 = 3(2) + b

5 = 6 + b

b = -1

Answer: y = 3x - 1

Example 4: Converting Standard Form to Slope-Intercept

Problem: What is the slope of the line 2x + 4y = 12?

Solution:

Convert to y = mx + b form:

2x + 4y = 12

4y = -2x + 12

y = -½x + 3

Answer: Slope = -½

Concept 3: Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

Understanding the relationship between slopes of parallel and perpendicular lines is crucial for many ACT problems.

Parallel and Perpendicular Line Rules

  • Parallel lines: Have the same slope (m₁ = m₂)
  • Perpendicular lines: Have negative reciprocal slopes (m₁ × m₂ = -1)

Example: If one line has slope 2, a perpendicular line has slope -½

Example 5: Finding a Parallel Line

Problem: What is the equation of a line parallel to y = 2x + 5 that passes through (3, 4)?

Solution:

Step 1: Parallel lines have the same slope, so m = 2

Step 2: Use y = mx + b with m = 2 and point (3, 4)

4 = 2(3) + b

4 = 6 + b

b = -2

Answer: y = 2x - 2

Example 6: Finding a Perpendicular Line

Problem: What is the slope of a line perpendicular to y = -3x + 7?

Solution:

The given line has slope m = -3

Perpendicular slope = negative reciprocal = -1/(-3) = ⅓

Answer: Slope = ⅓

Concept 4: Midpoint Formula

The midpoint is the point exactly halfway between two endpoints.

Midpoint Formula

M = ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)

Average the x-coordinates and average the y-coordinates

Example 7: Finding the Midpoint

Problem: What is the midpoint of the segment connecting (2, 5) and (8, 13)?

Solution:

M = ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)

M = ((2 + 8)/2, (5 + 13)/2)

M = (10/2, 18/2)

Answer: (5, 9)

Concept 5: Distance Formula

The distance formula calculates the length of a segment between two points. It's derived from the Pythagorean theorem.

Distance Formula

d = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]

This is the Pythagorean theorem in coordinate geometry!

Example 8: Finding Distance

Problem: What is the distance between points (1, 2) and (4, 6)?

Solution:

d = √[(x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²]

d = √[(4 - 1)² + (6 - 2)²]

d = √[3² + 4²]

d = √[9 + 16]

d = √25 = 5

Answer: 5 units

Recognize the 3-4-5 triangle!

Example 9: Complex Distance Problem

Problem: What is the distance between (-2, 3) and (3, -9)?

Solution:

d = √[(3 - (-2))² + (-9 - 3)²]

d = √[(3 + 2)² + (-12)²]

d = √[5² + 144]

d = √[25 + 144]

d = √169 = 13

Answer: 13 units

This is the 5-12-13 Pythagorean triple!

Example 10: Application Problem

Problem: A line passes through points A(1, 3) and B(5, 11). What is the equation of the line in slope-intercept form?

Solution:

Step 1: Find the slope

m = (11 - 3)/(5 - 1) = 8/4 = 2

Step 2: Use point-slope form with point A(1, 3)

y - 3 = 2(x - 1)

y - 3 = 2x - 2

y = 2x + 1

Answer: y = 2x + 1

ACT Strategy Tips

  • For parallel lines, just copy the slope from the given line
  • For perpendicular lines, flip the slope and change the sign
  • When finding distance, look for Pythagorean triples to save time
  • To find the y-intercept quickly, substitute x = 0 into the equation
  • Remember: horizontal lines have slope 0, vertical lines have undefined slope
