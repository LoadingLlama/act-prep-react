Concept 1: Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

The order of operations ensures everyone solves expressions the same way. This is fundamental to all algebra problems on the ACT.

PEMDAS/GEMDAS

  1. P/G - Parentheses/Grouping symbols first
  2. E - Exponents (including roots)
  3. MD - Multiplication and Division (left to right)
  4. AS - Addition and Subtraction (left to right)

Key point: Multiplication and division have equal priority. Same with addition and subtraction. Work left to right within each priority level!

Example 1: Basic PEMDAS

Problem: Simplify: 3 + 4 × 2

Solution:

Multiplication comes before addition

= 3 + (4 × 2)

= 3 + 8

Answer: 11

Common mistake: Don't do 3 + 4 = 7 first! That gives 14, which is wrong.

Example 2: Complex PEMDAS

Problem: Simplify: 20 - 12 ÷ 3 + 2²

Solution:

Step 1: Exponents first → 2² = 4

= 20 - 12 ÷ 3 + 4

Step 2: Division → 12 ÷ 3 = 4

= 20 - 4 + 4

Step 3: Subtraction and addition (left to right)

= 16 + 4

Answer: 20

Concept 2: Working with Negative Numbers

Negative numbers can be tricky! Master these rules to avoid common errors.

Rules for Negative Numbers

  • Adding a negative: Same as subtracting a positive (5 + (-3) = 5 - 3 = 2)
  • Subtracting a negative: Same as adding a positive (5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = 8)
  • Multiplying/Dividing:
    • Same signs → Positive result ((-4) × (-3) = 12)
    • Different signs → Negative result ((-4) × 3 = -12)

Example 3: Subtracting Negatives

Problem: Simplify: 7 - (-4)

Solution:

Subtracting a negative is the same as adding a positive

7 - (-4) = 7 + 4

Answer: 11

Example 4: Multiplying Negatives

Problem: Simplify: (-5) × (-3) + (-2) × 4

Solution:

Step 1: (-5) × (-3) = 15 (same signs = positive)

Step 2: (-2) × 4 = -8 (different signs = negative)

Step 3: 15 + (-8) = 15 - 8

Answer: 7

Concept 3: Combining Like Terms

Like terms have the same variable raised to the same power. You can only combine like terms by adding or subtracting their coefficients.

What are Like Terms?

  • Like terms: 3x and 5x, or 2x² and -7x²
  • NOT like terms: 3x and 3x², or 2x and 2y

Remember: Only the coefficient (number in front) changes when combining!

Example 5: Combining Like Terms

Problem: Simplify: 5x + 3 - 2x + 7

Solution:

Group like terms together:

= (5x - 2x) + (3 + 7)

= 3x + 10

Answer: 3x + 10

Example 6: Complex Combining

Problem: Simplify: 4x² - 2x + 5 + 3x - x² + 1

Solution:

Group by term types:

= (4x² - x²) + (-2x + 3x) + (5 + 1)

= 3x² + x + 6

Answer: 3x² + x + 6

Concept 4: Distributive Property

The distributive property lets you multiply a number or term by everything inside parentheses.

Distributive Property

a(b + c) = ab + ac

Multiply the outside term by each term inside the parentheses

Example 7: Basic Distribution

Problem: Simplify: 3(x + 5)

Solution:

Distribute the 3 to both terms:

= 3·x + 3·5

Answer: 3x + 15

Example 8: Negative Distribution

Problem: Simplify: -2(4x - 3)

Solution:

Distribute -2 to both terms:

= (-2)·(4x) + (-2)·(-3)

= -8x + 6

Answer: -8x + 6

Watch those signs! Negative times negative = positive

Concept 5: Solving Linear Equations

To solve equations, isolate the variable using inverse operations.

Steps to Solve Linear Equations

  1. Distribute to remove parentheses
  2. Combine like terms on each side
  3. Move variable terms to one side, constants to the other
  4. Divide or multiply to isolate the variable

Example 9: Multi-Step Equation

Problem: Solve for x: 2(x + 3) = 14

Solution:

Step 1: Distribute

2x + 6 = 14

Step 2: Subtract 6 from both sides

2x = 8

Step 3: Divide both sides by 2

Answer: x = 4

Example 10: Variables on Both Sides

Problem: Solve for x: 5x - 3 = 2x + 9

Solution:

Step 1: Subtract 2x from both sides

3x - 3 = 9

Step 2: Add 3 to both sides

3x = 12

Step 3: Divide by 3

Answer: x = 4

ACT Strategy Tips

  • Always check your work by plugging the answer back into the original equation
  • Write out your steps - don't try to do too much mental math
  • Watch for negative signs, especially when distributing
  • Remember: Whatever you do to one side, do to the other side
  • For PEMDAS problems, work slowly and carefully - one operation at a time
