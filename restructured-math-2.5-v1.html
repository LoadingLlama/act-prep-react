Conic sections (circles, ellipses, and hyperbolas) appear 1-3 times per ACT Math test, usually focusing on the equation of a circle in standard form. Understanding how to identify the center and radius from the equation is the most important skill for these questions.

1. Equation of a Circle

  • Standard form of a circle (MEMORIZE THIS):
    • (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r²
    • Center: (h, k)
    • Radius: r
  • Reading the center from the equation:
    • Example: (x - 3)² + (y + 5)² = 16 has center (3, -5)
    • Watch the signs! (x - h) means center x = +h, (y + k) means center y = -k
    • If (x - 3), the center x-coordinate is 3
    • If (y + 5), the center y-coordinate is -5 (opposite sign!)
  • Finding the radius:
    • r² is on the right side of the equation
    • Example: (x - 2)² + (y - 1)² = 25 has r² = 25, so r = 5
    • Example: (x + 1)² + (y - 3)² = 100 has r² = 100, so r = 10
    • Don't forget to take the square root!
  • Special case - circle centered at origin:
    • x² + y² = r²
    • Center: (0, 0)
    • Example: x² + y² = 49 is centered at origin with radius 7
  • Writing the equation when given center and radius:
    • Center (3, -2) and radius 5: (x - 3)² + (y + 2)² = 25
    • Center (-4, 7) and radius 6: (x + 4)² + (y - 7)² = 36
    • Remember to square the radius!

2. Completing the Square for Circles

  • Sometimes circle equations are given in expanded form:
    • Example: x² + 4x + y² - 8y + 5 = 0
    • This doesn't look like standard form!
    • You must complete the square to find center and radius
  • Steps to complete the square:
    • Step 1: Group x terms and y terms: (x² + 4x) + (y² - 8y) = -5
    • Step 2: For x terms, take half of coefficient of x, square it: (4/2)² = 4
    • Step 3: For y terms, take half of coefficient of y, square it: (-8/2)² = 16
    • Step 4: Add these to both sides: (x² + 4x + 4) + (y² - 8y + 16) = -5 + 4 + 16
    • Step 5: Factor: (x + 2)² + (y - 4)² = 15
    • Now you can read center (-2, 4) and radius √15
  • Quick formula for completing the square:
    • For x² + bx, add (b/2)²
    • This creates a perfect square: (x + b/2)²
    • Same process for y terms

3. Ellipses and Hyperbolas (Basic Recognition)

  • Ellipse standard form:
    • (x - h)²/a² + (y - k)²/b² = 1
    • Center: (h, k), just like a circle
    • Looks like a stretched or compressed circle
    • Both terms are added (+), both squared
    • Example: (x - 2)²/9 + (y + 1)²/4 = 1 is centered at (2, -1)
  • Hyperbola standard form:
    • (x - h)²/a² - (y - k)²/b² = 1 or (y - k)²/b² - (x - h)²/a² = 1
    • Center: (h, k)
    • Has two separate curved branches
    • Key difference: One term is subtracted (-)
    • Example: (x - 3)²/16 - (y + 2)²/9 = 1 is centered at (3, -2)
  • How to identify which conic section:
    • Circle: x² and y² have same coefficient, added together
    • Ellipse: x² and y² have different coefficients, added together
    • Hyperbola: x² and y² are subtracted
  • On the ACT:
    • Circles are tested most frequently
    • Ellipses and hyperbolas appear 0-2 times per test
    • Usually just need to identify center or general shape
    • Deep knowledge of ellipse/hyperbola properties rarely needed

4. Test-Taking Tips for Conic Sections

  • Master the circle equation first
    • (x - h)² + (y - k)² = r² is the most tested formula
    • Practice reading the center until automatic
    • Remember: opposite signs! (x - 3) means center x = 3
  • Watch for completing the square questions
    • If the equation isn't in standard form, you'll need to complete the square
    • Take half the coefficient, square it, add to both sides
    • Don't forget to move the constant to the other side first!
  • Use the signs to identify the conic
    • Both terms positive (+)? Circle or ellipse
    • One term negative (-)? Hyperbola
    • This quick check eliminates wrong answers fast
  • Radius is always positive
    • r² = 25 means r = 5 (not ±5)
    • Radius cannot be negative in geometry
  • Memorize the standard forms
    • Circle: (x-h)²+(y-k)²=r²
    • Ellipse: (x-h)²/a²+(y-k)²/b²=1
    • Hyperbola: (x-h)²/a²-(y-k)²/b²=1
    • These formulas are NOT provided on the test
