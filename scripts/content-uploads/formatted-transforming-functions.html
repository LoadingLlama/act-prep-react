On the ACT, you need to know how lines, parabolas, and other functions shift and transform in the coordinate plane. All functions follow the same transformation rules.

1. Transformation Rules

Four key rules govern how functions transform:

1. Inside parentheses → Horizontal shifts
Adding shifts LEFT, subtracting shifts RIGHT

2. Outside parentheses → Vertical shifts
Adding shifts UP, subtracting shifts DOWN

3. Negative sign in front → Vertical flip over x-axis

4. Coefficient in front → Vertical stretch/compression
Greater than 1 = stretch, less than 1 = compression

2. Horizontal Shifts

Numbers inside the parentheses cause horizontal shifts. The direction may seem backwards:

  • f(x − 3): Shifts 3 units RIGHT (subtracting shifts right)
  • f(x + 2): Shifts 2 units LEFT (adding shifts left)

3. Vertical Shifts

Numbers outside the parentheses cause vertical shifts. This follows intuition:

  • f(x) + 4: Shifts 4 units UP (adding shifts up)
  • f(x) − 3: Shifts 3 units DOWN (subtracting shifts down)

Example 1: Vertical Shift

The graph of 4x + 2y = 26 is shifted up 4 units. What is the y-coordinate of the resulting y-intercept?

A. 13
B. 17
C. 22
D. 30

Solution:

  • Convert to slope-intercept form: 2y = −4x + 26
  • Divide by 2: y = −2x + 13
  • Original y-intercept is at (0, 13)
  • Shift up 4 units: 13 + 4 = 17

Answer: B

4. Vertical Stretches and Compressions

A coefficient in front of the function causes vertical transformation:

  • 3f(x): Vertical stretch by factor of 3 (coefficient > 1)
  • (1/2)f(x): Vertical compression by factor of 1/2 (coefficient < 1)

5. Reflections

A negative sign in front causes the function to flip over the x-axis:

  • −f(x): Reflects over x-axis (flips vertically)
  • All points (x, y) become (x, −y)

6. Combining Transformations

Multiple transformations can be applied to the same function. For example:

  • f(x) = x² is the parent function
  • g(x) = 2(x − 3)² + 5 transforms it:
    • Shifts right 3 units (x − 3)
    • Stretches vertically by factor of 2
    • Shifts up 5 units (+5)

7. ACT Strategy Tips

Tip 1: Remember Horizontal Shifts Are Backwards

  • f(x − 5) shifts RIGHT 5, not left
    • Subtract inside parentheses = shift right
    • Add inside parentheses = shift left

Tip 2: Apply Transformations in Order

  • For complex transformations, apply in this order:
    • 1. Horizontal shifts (inside parentheses)
    • 2. Vertical stretch/compression/reflection
    • 3. Vertical shifts (outside)

.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Inside parentheses: f(x − a) shifts right, f(x + a) shifts left
  • ✓Outside parentheses: f(x) + a shifts up, f(x) − a shifts down
  • ✓Coefficient > 1: vertical stretch; coefficient < 1: vertical compression
  • ✓Negative sign in front: −f(x) reflects over x-axis
  • ✓These rules apply to ALL functions: lines, parabolas, cubics, etc.
