For the ACT, you need to know how to handle arithmetic, geometric, and recursive sequences. These are ordered lists of numbers that follow specific patterns.

1. Arithmetic Sequences

Arithmetic sequences use addition/subtraction between terms. The common difference (d) is constant.

Formula: t_n = t_1 + d(n − 1)

t_1 = first term
d = common difference
n = term number
t_n = nth term

Example: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24...

  • Common difference d = 4 (adding 4 each time)
  • To find 88th term: t_88 = 4 + 4(88 − 1) = 352

Example: Finding Terms

In an arithmetic sequence, the 1st and 4th terms are 3 and 24. What is the 5th term?

A. 27
B. 30
C. 31
D. 37

Solution:

  • Draw diagram: 3, __, __, 24, __
  • From 1st to 4th term = 3 jumps of d
  • Total difference: 24 − 3 = 21
  • Common difference: d = 21 ÷ 3 = 7
  • 5th term: 24 + 7 = 31

Answer: C

2. Geometric Sequences

Geometric sequences use multiplication/division between terms. The common ratio (r) is constant.

Formula: t_n = t_1 × r^(n − 1)

r = common ratio
t_n = nth term

Example: 3, 6, 12, 24, 48, 96...

  • Common ratio r = 2 (multiplying by 2 each time)
  • To find ratio: divide any term by previous term
  • Example: 6 ÷ 3 = 2

Example: Negative Ratio

The 1st term is 560 and 2nd term is −280. What is the 5th term?

A. 70
B. 35
C. 17.5
D. −35

Solution:

  • Common ratio: −280 ÷ 560 = −0.5
  • 3rd term: −280 × (−0.5) = 140
  • 4th term: 140 × (−0.5) = −70
  • 5th term: −70 × (−0.5) = 35

Answer: B

3. Key Differences

  • Arithmetic: Add/subtract same value each time (common difference)
  • Geometric: Multiply/divide by same value each time (common ratio)
  • Finding d: Subtract consecutive terms
  • Finding r: Divide any term by previous term

4. ACT Strategy Tips

Tip 1: Draw Diagrams

  • Always draw out the sequence
    • Fill in known values
    • Count jumps between terms
    • Visualize the pattern

Tip 2: Use Formulas for Large Term Numbers

  • For small terms (5th, 6th), can count manually
  • For large terms (88th, 100th), must use formula

.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Arithmetic: t_n = t_1 + d(n − 1) where d is common difference
  • ✓Geometric: t_n = t_1 × r^(n − 1) where r is common ratio
  • ✓Find d by subtracting terms; find r by dividing terms
  • ✓Always draw diagrams to visualize sequences
  • ✓Use formulas for finding distant terms efficiently
