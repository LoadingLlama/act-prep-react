Arcs and sectors questions occasionally appear on the ACT. This topic is not as commonly tested as other geometry concepts, but it is one that you should still be familiar with for test day. Understanding how to find arc lengths and sector areas will help you tackle these specialized circle problems.

1. Understanding Arcs

An arc is a portion of a circle's circumference. Arcs can be measured in two ways:

  • Degree measure: equal to the central angle
    • If central angle is 90°, the arc measure is 90°
  • Arc length: the actual distance along the arc
    • Measured in units (inches, cm, etc.)

Arc Length Formula

Arc Length / Circumference = θ / 360°
or L = (θ/360°) × 2πr
where θ = central angle, r = radius

Example 1: Finding Central Angle from Arc Length

In a circle with radius 8, the length of minor arc BC is 6π. What is the measure of the central angle in degrees?

A. 270
B. 180
C. 135
D. 100

Solution:

  • Use formula: 6π / (2π × 8) = θ / 360°
  • Simplify: 6π / 16π = θ / 360°
  • 3/8 = θ / 360° → θ = (3/8) × 360° = 135°

Answer: C

2. Sectors and Area

A sector is a portion of a circle's area, like a slice of pie. Think of it as the region between two radii and an arc.

Sector Area Formula

Sector Area / Circle Area = θ / 360°
or S = (θ/360°) × πr²
where S = sector area, θ = central angle, r = radius

Example 2: Finding Sector Area

For a circle with radius 6, if the central angle is 150°, what is the area of the sector?

A. 8π
B. 10π
C. 12π
D. 15π

Solution:

  • Use formula: S = (150°/360°) × π(6)²
  • S = (150/360) × 36π = (15/36) × 36π
  • S = 15π

Answer: D

3. Proportions and Real-World Applications

The ACT often tests arcs and sectors using proportions. The key insight: the fraction of the circle equals the fraction of 360°.

Proportion Method

  • Part / Whole = Angle / 360°
    • If 5 out of 25 students choose strawberry, sector angle = (5/25) × 360° = 72°
    • If eating 2 out of 12 pizza slices, you ate (2/12) of the pizza

Example 3: Pie Chart Application

A pie chart represents favorite ice cream flavors. If 5 out of 25 students selected strawberry, what is the central angle for the strawberry sector?

A. 72°
B. 80°
C. 95°
D. 108°

Solution:

  • Set up proportion: 5/25 = θ/360°
  • 1/5 = θ/360° → θ = 360° ÷ 5 = 72°

Answer: A

4. Inscribed Angle Theorem

An inscribed angle has its vertex on the circle with sides as chords.

Key Rule

  • For an inscribed angle and central angle intersecting the same arc:
    • Inscribed angle = ½ × Central angle
    • If central angle is 90°, inscribed angle is 45°

5. ACT Strategy Tips

Tip 1: Use Proportions

  • Most arc/sector problems can be solved with simple fractions:
    • Part/Whole = Arc/Circumference = Sector/Circle Area = Angle/360°
    • All four fractions are equal
    • Pick the easiest one to work with

Tip 2: Memorize the Two Main Formulas

  • Arc length: L = (θ/360°) × 2πr
  • Sector area: S = (θ/360°) × πr²
  • Notice both use (θ/360°) as the fraction

Tip 3: Draw and Label

  • When no figure is given:
    • Sketch a quick circle
    • Draw the central angle
    • Label known values
    • Visualizing helps avoid errors
