The ACT loves to ask geometry questions, and you will see them throughout the math test. Most commonly, you will need to know how to find the area and volume of various shapes or apply the rules for various types of triangles. As long as you have the equations memorized, you will be ready to solve any geometry questions with shapes.

1. Essential Area and Volume Formulas

You will not be given any equations on the ACT, so you need to memorize all of the formulas below. These are the most commonly tested shapes on the test.

Two-Dimensional Shapes

  • Triangle: A = ½bh
    • b = base, h = height (perpendicular to base)
  • Rectangle: A = lw, Perimeter = 2l + 2w
    • l = length, w = width
  • Square: A = s², Perimeter = 4s
  • Parallelogram: A = bh
  • Trapezoid: A = ½(b₁ + b₂)h
  • Circle: A = πr², Circumference = 2πr
    • r = radius (distance from center to edge)

Three-Dimensional Solids

  • Rectangular Prism: V = lwh
  • Cube: V = s³
  • Right Cylinder: V = πr²h
  • General 3D Solid: V = B × h
    • B = area of the base, h = height
    • This works for any three-dimensional shape

Example 1: Equal Areas

One side of rectangle ABCD has a length of 9 inches. A square whose area is equal to the area of rectangle ABCD has a side length of 12 inches. What is the width, in inches, of rectangle ABCD?

A. 9
B. 12
C. 16
D. 18

Solution:

  • Find the area of the square: A = 12² = 144
  • The rectangle has the same area, so: 144 = 9w
  • Solve for width: w = 144 ÷ 9 = 16

Answer: C

2. Unit Conversions for Area and Volume

The ACT frequently tests unit conversion questions. The most important strategy: convert units BEFORE solving for any area or volume. Students often answer these questions incorrectly because they convert units after calculating.

Essential Unit Conversions

  • Linear: 1 yard = 3 feet, 1 foot = 12 inches
  • Area: 1 square yard = 9 square feet, 1 square foot = 144 square inches
  • Volume: 1 cubic yard = 27 cubic feet, 1 cubic foot = 1,728 cubic inches

Example 2: Unit Conversion

A professional soccer field is 136 yards long and 93 yards wide. What is the area of the soccer field in square feet?

A. 113,832
B. 37,944
C. 12,648
D. 4,216

Solution:

  • Convert FIRST: 136 yards × 3 = 408 feet, 93 yards × 3 = 279 feet
  • Calculate area: A = 408 × 279 = 113,832 ft²

Answer: A

3. Right Triangles and Pythagorean Theorem

All right triangles follow the Pythagorean Theorem: a² + b² = c², where a and b are the legs and c is the hypotenuse (the longest side opposite the right angle).

Pythagorean Triples

  • 3-4-5 triangle: Most common triple
    • Can be scaled: 6-8-10, 9-12-15, 12-16-20, etc.
  • 5-12-13 triangle: Second most common
    • Can be scaled: 10-24-26, 15-36-39, etc.

Special Right Triangles

  • 45°-45°-90° triangle: sides are in ratio x : x : x√2
    • Also called a right isosceles triangle
    • Two legs are equal, hypotenuse is leg × √2
  • 30°-60°-90° triangle: sides are in ratio x : x√3 : 2x
    • Short leg : long leg : hypotenuse
    • Short leg is always half the hypotenuse

Example 3: Special Right Triangle

In a 30°-60°-90° triangle, y = 10. What is the value of x (the shortest side)?

A. 3
B. 5
C. 8
D. 10

Solution:

  • The shortest side (x) is always half the hypotenuse (y)
  • x = ½(10) = 5

Answer: B

4. Similar Triangles

Similar triangles have the same shape but different sizes. Their angles are identical, and their side lengths are proportional.

Key Properties

  • All corresponding angles are equal
  • Ratio of corresponding sides is constant:
    • AB/DE = BC/EF = AC/DF
  • When a line is drawn parallel to one side of a triangle, it creates a smaller similar triangle

Example 4: Similar Triangles with Parallel Line

In triangle ABC, DE is parallel to BC, AE = 8, AC = 20, and AB = 18. What is the length of AD?

A. 4.1
B. 7.2
C. 8.8
D. 12.2

Solution:

  • Triangles ADE and ABC are similar (DE is parallel to BC)
  • Set up proportion: AD/AB = AE/AC
  • AD/18 = 8/20 → 20(AD) = 144 → AD = 7.2

Answer: B

5. Triangle Rules and Properties

Third Side Rule

The sum of the two shorter sides of a triangle must be greater than the longest side: a + b > c

Triangle Types

  • Equilateral triangle: all three sides equal, all angles are 60°
  • Isosceles triangle: two equal sides, two equal angles
    • Equal sides are opposite equal angles
  • Acute triangle: all angles less than 90°
  • Obtuse triangle: one angle greater than 90°
  • Right triangle: one angle equals 90°

6. ACT Strategy Tips

Tip 1: Memorize All Formulas

  • No formulas are provided on the ACT
    • Create flashcards for all area and volume formulas
    • Practice special right triangle ratios until automatic
    • Know Pythagorean triples by heart

Tip 2: Convert Units First

  • Always convert units BEFORE calculating area or volume
    • This prevents the most common unit conversion errors
    • Square units: multiply by conversion factor squared
    • Cubic units: multiply by conversion factor cubed

Tip 3: Use Figures for Educated Guesses

  • ACT figures are drawn to scale (unless noted otherwise)
    • Estimate side lengths visually
    • Eliminate obviously wrong answer choices
    • Compare answer choices to the figure
