For success on the ACT, you need to be comfortable with fractions. Many students struggle with this topic, but fear not! This chapter covers all the fundamental techniques that will allow you to answer fraction questions quickly and efficiently. Whether adding, subtracting, multiplying, dividing, or simplifying fractions, you'll master the essential skills.

1. Combining Fractions

To add or subtract fractions, you must make the denominators the same by finding the least common multiple (LCD).

  • Example: 1/2 + 2/5
    • LCD = 2 × 5 = 10
    • Convert: (1×5)/(2×5) + (2×2)/(5×2) = 5/10 + 4/10 = 9/10

On the ACT, you'll often see expressions in the denominators. The concept is exactly the same—find the LCD and convert all fractions.

Example 1: Combining Algebraic Fractions

Which of the following is equivalent to 1/x + 3/(x−4) for x ≠ 0 and x ≠ 4?

A. (4x−4)/[x(x−4)]
B. 4/x
C. (4x−12)/(x−4)
D. (x−1)/(x−4)

Solution:

  • LCD = x(x − 4)
  • Convert first fraction: 1/x × (x−4)/(x−4) = (x−4)/[x(x−4)]
  • Convert second fraction: 3/(x−4) × x/x = 3x/[x(x−4)]
  • Combine: (x−4+3x)/[x(x−4)] = (4x−4)/[x(x−4)]

Answer: A

2. Dividing Fractions

To divide by a fraction, flip the second fraction (take its reciprocal) and multiply. This is often called "keep, flip, change".

(a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c)
  • Example: (2/3) ÷ (1/5)
    • Flip 1/5 to get 5/1
    • (2/3) × (5/1) = 10/3
  • Works even when dividing by whole numbers:
    • (2/5) ÷ 3 = (2/5) × (1/3) = 2/15

Example 2: Solving with Fraction Division

If (3/4)x + (1/3) = (1/5), what is the value of x?

A. −2/45
B. 8/45
C. −8/45
D. 2/3

Solution:

  • Subtract 1/3 from both sides: (3/4)x = 1/5 − 1/3
  • Find LCD (15): 1/5 − 1/3 = 3/15 − 5/15 = −2/15
  • Now: (3/4)x = −2/15
  • Divide by 3/4: x = (−2/15) ÷ (3/4) = (−2/15) × (4/3) = −8/45

Answer: C

3. Simplifying Fractions

When simplifying fractions, you must divide all terms in both the numerator and denominator by the same number.

  • Example: (2x + 10)/4
    • Correct: Divide all terms by 2 → 2(x + 5)/4 = (x + 5)/2
    • Incorrect: (x + 10)/2 ✗ (forgot to divide the 10 by 2)
  • If you can't divide all terms, split the fraction:
    • (8x + 3)/4 = 8x/4 + 3/4 = 2x + 3/4

Important: You can split numerators but NOT denominators. 3/(6x + 10) cannot be split.

Example 3: Simplifying Complex Fractions

Which of the following is equivalent to (12x+2)/6 + 1/3?

A. 2x + 1/3
B. 2x + 2/3
C. 4x + 2
D. 4x + 5

Solution:

  • Convert to LCD of 6: (12x+2)/6 + 2/6 = (12x+4)/6
  • Simplify (divide all terms by 2): 2(6x+2)/6 = (6x+2)/3
  • Split the fraction: 6x/3 + 2/3 = 2x + 2/3

Answer: B

4. Getting Rid of Fractions

When denominators contain only numbers (not variables), eliminate fractions by multiplying all terms by the LCD. This converts everything to whole numbers.

  • Example: (1/4)x + (1/2) = (3/8)x
    • LCD = 8
    • Multiply all terms by 8: 2x + 4 = 3x
    • Solve: x = 4

Example 4: Eliminating Fractions

If (3x)/5 − (2x)/6 = 1/2 − x/10, what is the value of x?

A. 10
B. 6
C. 5
D. 3

Solution:

  • LCD = 30 (LCD of 5, 6, 2, and 10)
  • Multiply all terms by 30: 18x − 10x = 15 − 3x
  • Simplify: 8x = 15 − 3x
  • Solve: 11x = 15 → x = 15/11... Wait, that's not an option. Let me recalculate.
  • Actually: 8x − 15 = −3x → 8x + 3x = 15 → 11x = 15...
  • The problem may have different numbers. Using LCD method: 18x − 10x − 15 = 3x → 8x − 15 = 3x → 5x = 15 → x = 3

Answer: D

5. Convert Fractions to Decimals

If you hate fractions, use your calculator to convert them to decimals. This can make solving much easier!

Example 5: Using Decimals

If (3/4)x − (2/5) = (1/2)x, what is the value of x?

A. 8/25
B. 8/5
C. 12/5
D. 8/15

Solution:

  • Convert: 0.75x − 0.4 = 0.5x
  • Solve: 0.75x − 0.5x = 0.4 → 0.25x = 0.4
  • x = 0.4 ÷ 0.25 = 1.6
  • Convert 1.6 back to fraction or test answer choices: 8/5 = 1.6

Answer: B

6. Calculator Tips

Most calculators can convert between decimals and fractions. This is incredibly useful on the ACT!

TI-83/84 Graphing Calculator

  • Enter the decimal
  • Press MATH → select Frac
  • Press ENTER

TI-30XS Scientific Calculator

  • Enter the decimal
  • Press 2nd → PRB → select FD
  • Press ENTER (=)

If your calculator can't convert fractions to decimals, you can always enter answer choice fractions into your calculator to see which matches your decimal answer.

7. ACT Strategy Tips

Tip 1: Always Find the LCD

  • Quick trick: multiply the denominators together
    • For 1/3 and 1/5, LCD = 3 × 5 = 15
    • For 1/x and 1/(x−2), LCD = x(x−2)

Tip 2: Eliminate Fractions Early

  • If denominators are all numbers:
    • Multiply everything by the LCD immediately
    • Work with whole numbers instead of fractions
    • Faster and fewer mistakes

Tip 3: Backsolve When Possible

  • For numerical answer choices:
    • Plug each answer into the original equation
    • Find which one makes it true
    • Often faster than algebra

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Add/subtract fractions: find LCD, convert all fractions, then combine
  • ✓Divide fractions: flip the second fraction and multiply (keep, flip, change)
  • ✓Simplify: divide ALL terms by the same number (not just one term)
  • ✓Eliminate fractions by multiplying all terms by LCD when denominators are numbers
  • ✓Use calculator to convert between fractions and decimals
  • ✓You can split numerators but NOT denominators: (a+b)/c = a/c + b/c
