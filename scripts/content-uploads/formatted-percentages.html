Percentages are a fundamental concept on the ACT. You need to handle simple percentages and percentage increases/decreases efficiently.

1. Simple Percentages

Proportion: new/original = %/100

Or: new = original × (% / 100)

Example: Finding Value

Isaiah bought a watch for 72% of the $160 original price. How much did he pay?

Solution:

  • Set up: x/160 = 72/100
  • Or: x = 160 × 0.72 = $115.20

Answer: $115

2. Percentage Increase/Decrease

% change = (new − original) / original × 100

Increase: multiply by (1 + %)
Decrease: multiply by (1 − %)

3. ACT Tips

  • Convert to decimal: 72% = 0.72
  • 25% increase: multiply by 1.25
  • 15% decrease: multiply by 0.85

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Use proportion: new/original = %/100
  • ✓Increase: multiply by (1 + %), decrease: multiply by (1 − %)
  • ✓% change = (new − original) / original × 100
