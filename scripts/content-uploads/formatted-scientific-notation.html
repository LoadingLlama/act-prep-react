Scientific notation expresses very large or small numbers compactly as a × 10^n.

1. Format

a × 10^n

where 1 ≤ a < 10
n = number of decimal places moved

2. Converting

  • Large numbers: positive exponent (5,000,000 = 5 × 10^6)
  • Small numbers: negative exponent (0.00003 = 3 × 10^-5)
  • Move decimal right → decrease exponent
  • Move decimal left → increase exponent

3. Operations

  • Multiply: multiply coefficients, add exponents
  • Divide: divide coefficients, subtract exponents
  • Example: (2×10^3) × (3×10^4) = 6×10^7

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Format: a × 10^n where 1 ≤ a < 10
  • ✓Positive exponent = large, negative = small
  • ✓Multiply: add exponents; Divide: subtract exponents
