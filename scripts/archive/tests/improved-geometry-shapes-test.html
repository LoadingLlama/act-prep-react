Every geometry problem on the ACT comes down to knowing the right formula. In this lesson, we'll learn the area and perimeter formulas for all common shapes—with clear visuals to help you remember them. By the end, you'll know exactly which formula to use for any shape!

Area vs. Perimeter: The Fundamental Difference

Perimeter Area
Definition Distance around the shape Space inside the shape
Units Linear (cm, m, ft) Square (cm², m², ft²)
Think of it as... Fence around a yard Grass inside the yard

Rectangles and Squares

Rectangle

Perimeter: P = 2l + 2w Area: A = l × w

Example: Rectangle with length 8 and width 5

  • Perimeter: P = 2(8) + 2(5) = 16 + 10 = 26
  • Area: A = 8 × 5 = 40

Square (Special Rectangle)

Perimeter: P = 4s Area: A = s²

Example: Square with side 6

  • Perimeter: P = 4(6) = 24
  • Area: A = 6² = 36

Triangles

For any triangle, the area formula is the same:

A = ½ × base × height

A = ½bh

⚠️ Critical: The height must be perpendicular (90°) to the base!

Example: Triangle with base 10 and height 6

  • Area: A = ½ × 10 × 6 = ½ × 60 = 30

Circles

Key term: The radius (r) is the distance from the center to the edge.

Circumference: C = 2πr Area: A = πr²

Remember: π ≈ 3.14 (or use the π button on your calculator)

Example: Circle with radius 5

  • Circumference: C = 2π(5) = 10π ≈ 31.4
  • Area: A = π(5)² = 25π ≈ 78.5

Trapezoids

A trapezoid has two parallel sides (bases) of different lengths:

A = ½h(b₁ + b₂)

Think of it as: Average the two bases, then multiply by height!

Example: Trapezoid with b₁ = 6, b₂ = 10, h = 4

  • Area: A = ½(4)(6 + 10) = ½(4)(16) = 2(16) = 32

Formula Summary

Shape Perimeter/Circumference Area
Rectangle P = 2l + 2w A = lw
Square P = 4s A = s²
Triangle P = a + b + c A = ½bh
Circle C = 2πr A = πr²
Trapezoid P = a + b + c + d A = ½h(b₁ + b₂)

Example Problem

ACT-Style Question

Problem: A rectangle has length 12 cm and width 5 cm. What is its area?

A. 17 cm²
B. 34 cm²
C. 60 cm²
D. 120 cm²

Solution: Use the rectangle area formula.

Step 1: Identify the formula: A = l × w

Step 2: Plug in values: A = 12 × 5

Step 3: Calculate: A = 60 cm²

Answer: C

Key Takeaway

Problem-Solving Strategy:

  1. Identify the shape (rectangle, circle, triangle, etc.)
  2. Choose the right formula (perimeter or area?)
  3. Plug in the values carefully
  4. Calculate and check units (linear for perimeter, square for area)

Pro tip: Write down the formula first before plugging in numbers. This helps catch mistakes and shows your work clearly. For area, you're always multiplying dimensions. For perimeter, you're adding them!
