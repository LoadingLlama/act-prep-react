Lines are fundamental to coordinate geometry on the ACT. You'll need to know how to find slopes, write equations of lines, calculate midpoints, and find distances between points. These concepts appear frequently throughout the math section.

Slope

The slope of a line measures how steep it is. Given two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂), the slope formula is:

m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)

rise over run

Key slope rules:

  • Positive slope: line goes up from left to right
  • Negative slope: line goes down from left to right
  • Zero slope: horizontal line
  • Undefined slope: vertical line
  • Parallel lines have equal slopes
  • Perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals (m₁ = -1/m₂)

Equations of Lines

There are several forms for the equation of a line. You should know all of them:

Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + b

m = slope
b = y-intercept

Point-Slope Form

y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

Use when you know slope and a point

Standard Form

Ax + By = C

A, B, C are constants

Midpoint Formula

The midpoint between two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) is:

Midpoint = ((x₁ + x₂)/2, (y₁ + y₂)/2)

Average the x-coordinates and y-coordinates

Distance Formula

The distance between two points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) is:

d = √[(x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²]

Based on the Pythagorean theorem

Example Problems

Example 1

Problem:

What is the slope of the line that passes through the points (−3, 7) and (5, −1)?

A. -1
B. -1/2
C. 1/2
D. 1

Solution:

We'll use the slope formula with (x₁, y₁) = (−3, 7) and (x₂, y₂) = (5, −1).

m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)
m = (−1 − 7) / (5 − (−3))
m = −8 / 8
m = −1

Answer: A

Example 2

Problem:

A line passes through the point (2, 5) and has a slope of 3. Which of the following is an equation of this line?

A. y = 3x − 1
B. y = 3x + 1
C. y = 3x − 11
D. y = 3x + 11

Solution:

We can use point-slope form or plug the point into slope-intercept form. Let's use y = mx + b with m = 3.

y = 3x + b
5 = 3(2) + b
5 = 6 + b
b = −1
y = 3x − 1

Answer: A

Example 3

Problem:

What is the distance between the points (1, 4) and (7, 12)?

A. 6
B. 8
C. 10
D. 14

Solution:

We'll use the distance formula.

d = √[(x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²]
d = √[(7 − 1)² + (12 − 4)²]
d = √[6² + 8²]
d = √[36 + 64]
d = √100 = 10

Answer: C

💡 Key Takeaway

Master the slope formula, equation forms, midpoint formula, and distance formula. Remember that parallel lines have equal slopes, while perpendicular lines have slopes that are negative reciprocals of each other.
