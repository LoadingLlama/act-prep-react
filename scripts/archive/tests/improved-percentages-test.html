Percentages show up everywhere on the ACT—from calculating discounts to solving word problems. Once you understand that "percent" simply means "per 100," everything else falls into place. Let's build your percentage skills step-by-step!

What Does "Percent" Mean?

Percent = "per 100"

When you see 25%, it literally means "25 per 100" or "25 out of 100." This is the foundation of all percentage problems.

As a fraction: 25% = 25/100 = 1/4

As a decimal: 25% = 0.25

Converting Between Forms

You need to move fluently between percentages, decimals, and fractions. Here's how:

Percent → Decimal

Rule: Move the decimal point 2 places to the LEFT and remove the % symbol

Examples:

  • 25% → Move decimal left 2: 0.25
  • 7.5% → Move decimal left 2: 0.075
  • 150% → Move decimal left 2: 1.50

Decimal → Percent

Rule: Move the decimal point 2 places to the RIGHT and add the % symbol

Examples:

  • 0.45 → Move decimal right 2: 45%
  • 0.08 → Move decimal right 2: 8%
  • 1.2 → Move decimal right 2: 120%

Fraction → Percent

Rule: Divide numerator by denominator, then convert to percent

Examples:

  • 3/4 = 0.75 = 75%
  • 1/5 = 0.2 = 20%
  • 7/8 = 0.875 = 87.5%

Finding a Percentage of a Number

This is the most common percentage calculation on the ACT.

Key Formula: To find X% of a number, convert X% to a decimal and multiply

Example: What is 35% of 80?

Step 1: Convert 35% to decimal
35% = 0.35

Step 2: Multiply by the number
0.35 × 80 = 28

Answer: 28

Real-world application: If a $80 item is 35% off, you save $28!

Percent Increase and Decrease

These problems ask: "By what percentage did something change?"

Percent Change Formula:

Percent Change = (Difference / Original) × 100%

Percent Increase

Example: A price goes from $40 to $50. What's the percent increase?

Step 1: Find the difference
New - Old = 50 - 40 = 10

Step 2: Divide by original and multiply by 100%
(10 / 40) × 100% = 0.25 × 100% = 25% increase

Percent Decrease

Example: A price drops from $50 to $40. What's the percent decrease?

Step 1: Find the difference
Old - New = 50 - 40 = 10

Step 2: Divide by original and multiply by 100%
(10 / 50) × 100% = 0.20 × 100% = 20% decrease

⚠️ Critical Rule: Always divide by the ORIGINAL value, not the new value!

Example Problem

ACT-Style Question

Problem: What is 20% of 150?

A. 15
B. 25
C. 30
D. 40

Solution: Convert 20% to decimal and multiply.

Step 1: Convert: 20% = 0.20

Step 2: Multiply: 0.20 × 150 = 30

Answer: C

Key Takeaway

Three Essential Skills:

  1. Conversions: Move decimal 2 places (left for %, right for decimal)
  2. Finding X% of Y: Convert to decimal and multiply
  3. Percent Change: (Difference / Original) × 100% — always use ORIGINAL value!

Master these three skills and you'll handle any percentage problem on the ACT. Practice moving between fractions, decimals, and percents until it becomes automatic!
