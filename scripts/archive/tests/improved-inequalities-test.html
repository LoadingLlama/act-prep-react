Inequalities are similar to equations, but instead of showing equality, they show relationships like "greater than" or "less than." Master inequalities by understanding the symbols, learning the one critical rule, and practicing the solving process step-by-step.

Understanding Inequality Symbols

There are four inequality symbols you need to know:

Symbol Meaning Example
> Greater than x > 5 means x is more than 5
≥ Greater than or equal to x ≥ 5 means x is 5 or more
< Less than x < 5 means x is less than 5
≤ Less than or equal to x ≤ 5 means x is 5 or less

Memory tip: The symbol always "points" to the smaller value. Think of it as an alligator mouth that wants to eat the bigger number!

The ONE Critical Rule

⚠️ FLIP THE INEQUALITY SIGN when you multiply or divide BOTH sides by a NEGATIVE number!

This is the most common mistake students make. Everything else works exactly like equations, but this rule is unique to inequalities.

Why does this happen? When you multiply by a negative, you reverse the order of numbers on a number line. For example:

  • If 3 < 5 (3 is less than 5)
  • Then -3 > -5 (negative 3 is GREATER than negative 5)

Solving Inequalities: Step-by-Step Process

Solve inequalities just like equations—isolate the variable using inverse operations. The only difference is remembering to flip the sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative.

Example 1: Basic Inequality (No Sign Flip)

Problem: Solve 3x + 7 > 16

Step 1: Subtract 7 from both sides

3x + 7 - 7 > 16 - 7
3x > 9

Step 2: Divide both sides by 3

3x/3 > 9/3
x > 3

Solution: x > 3 (No sign flip because we divided by positive 3)

Example 2: Inequality with Sign Flip

Problem: Solve -2x + 5 < 13

Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides

-2x + 5 - 5 < 13 - 5
-2x < 8

Step 2: Divide both sides by -2 and FLIP the sign!

-2x/-2 > 8/-2
x > -4

Solution: x > -4 (Sign flipped from < to > because we divided by -2)

Graphing Inequalities on a Number Line

Visual representation helps you understand the solution set:

x > 3: Open circle, shade right 3 x ≥ 3: Filled circle, shade right 3 x < 3: Open circle, shade left 3 x ≤ 3: Filled circle, shade left

Circle Rules:

  • Open circle (○): Use for < or > (does NOT include the number)
  • Filled circle (●): Use for ≤ or ≥ (DOES include the number)

Example Problem

ACT-Style Question

Problem: Solve for x: -3x + 7 > 16

A. x > -3
B. x < -3
C. x > 3
D. x < 3

Solution: Follow the steps carefully and remember to flip the sign when dividing by -3.

Step 1: Subtract 7 from both sides
-3x + 7 - 7 > 16 - 7
-3x > 9

Step 2: Divide by -3 and FLIP the sign
-3x/-3 < 9/-3
x < -3

Answer: B

Common mistake: Forgetting to flip the sign would give x > -3 (answer A), which is wrong!

Key Takeaway

Three-Step Checklist for Inequalities:

  1. Solve like a normal equation (isolate the variable)
  2. Check if you multiply/divide by a negative → FLIP the sign if yes
  3. Graph your answer to verify it makes sense

The #1 mistake is forgetting to flip the sign. To avoid this, always ask yourself: "Did I multiply or divide by a negative?" If yes, flip it! Practice this until it becomes automatic.
