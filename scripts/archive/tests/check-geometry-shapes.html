Geometry questions appear frequently on the ACT Math test. You'll need to know formulas for area, perimeter, and volume of various shapes, as well as special rules for right triangles. Memorize all the bolded formulas in this lesson—they won't be provided on the test!

Area Formulas

You must memorize the area formulas for common shapes. Here are the most important ones:

Triangle

A = ½bh

b = base, h = height

Rectangle

A = lw

l = length, w = width

Perimeter = 2l + 2w

Square

A = s²

s = side length

Perimeter = 4s

Circle

A = πr²

r = radius

Circumference = 2πr

Parallelogram

A = bh

b = base, h = height

Trapezoid

A = ½(b₁ + b₂)h

b₁, b₂ = parallel bases, h = height

Volume Formulas

You also need to know volume formulas for 3D shapes:

Rectangular Prism

V = lwh

l = length, w = width, h = height

Surface Area = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

Cube

V = s³

s = side length

Surface Area = 6s²

Cylinder

V = πr²h

r = radius, h = height

Example Problems

Example 1

Problem:

One side of rectangle ABCD has a length of 9 inches. A square whose area is equal to the area of rectangle ABCD has a side length of 12 inches. What is the width, in inches, of rectangle ABCD?

A. 3
B. 12
C. 16
D. 21

Solution:

Since the area of rectangle ABCD and the square are equal, we first find the area of the square.

A = s²
A = 12² = 144 square inches

Now we know the area of rectangle ABCD is 144 square inches and one side length is 9 inches. We can solve for the width using A = lw.

144 = 9w
w = 16

Answer: C

Example 2

Problem:

Amanda is making a cylindrical ice cream cake. The pan has a diameter of 25 cm. If Amanda uses 2,000 cm³ of ice cream to make the cake, which of the following is closest to the height, in centimeters, of the ice cream cake?

A. 10
B. 8
C. 6
D. 4

Solution:

We'll use the volume formula for a cylinder. We know the volume is 2,000 cm³. The radius is half the diameter, so r = 12.5 cm.

V = πr²h
2000 = π(12.5)²h
2000 = π(156.25)h
h = 2000 / (π × 156.25)
h ≈ 4.07 cm

The height is approximately 4.07 cm, which is closest to 4.

Answer: D

Right Triangles

Right triangles have special properties you must know for the ACT.

Pythagorean Theorem

For any right triangle: a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse (longest side) and a and b are the legs.

Special Right Triangles

30-60-90 Triangle

Side ratios: 1 : √3 : 2

If the short leg = x, then:
Long leg = x√3
Hypotenuse = 2x

45-45-90 Triangle

Side ratios: 1 : 1 : √2

If each leg = x, then:
Hypotenuse = x√2

Example 3

Problem:

In a 30-60-90 triangle, the side opposite the 30° angle has a length of 7. What is the length of the hypotenuse?

A. 7
B. 7√2
C. 7√3
D. 14

Solution:

In a 30-60-90 triangle, the side opposite the 30° angle is the shortest side. Using the ratio 1 : √3 : 2, if the short side = 7, then the hypotenuse = 2 × 7 = 14.

Answer: D

💡 Key Takeaway

Memorize all area and volume formulas—they won't be given to you on the ACT. Pay special attention to the special right triangles (30-60-90 and 45-45-90), as they appear frequently and save you time over using the Pythagorean theorem.
