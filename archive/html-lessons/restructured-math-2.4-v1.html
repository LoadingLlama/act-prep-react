Arcs and sectors appear occasionally on the ACT Math test, usually 1-2 questions per test. These problems test your understanding of how parts of a circle relate to the whole circle using proportional reasoning with central angles.

1. Understanding Arcs

  • An arc is a portion of a circle's circumference
    • Think of it as a "slice" of the circle's edge
    • Arcs are measured in two ways: degree measure or arc length
  • Degree measure of an arc:
    • Equal to the measure of the central angle that intercepts it
    • A central angle is an angle whose vertex is at the center of the circle
    • Example: If the central angle is 90°, the arc measures 90°
  • Arc length (the actual distance along the curve):
    • Arc length is a portion of the total circumference
    • Use the proportion: arc length / circumference = angle / 360°
  • Arc length formula (MEMORIZE THIS):
    • Arc Length = (θ/360°) × 2πr
    • Where θ = central angle in degrees
    • r = radius of the circle
    • 2πr = circumference of the whole circle
  • Alternative form (same formula):
    • Arc Length / Circumference = θ / 360°
    • This proportion shows: arc is to whole circle as angle is to 360°
    • Useful when you need to solve for the angle or radius

2. Understanding Sectors

  • A sector is a "slice" of a circle (like a pizza slice)
    • Bounded by two radii and an arc
    • Sectors have area, just like the whole circle has area
  • Sector area formula (MEMORIZE THIS):
    • Sector Area = (θ/360°) × πr²
    • Where θ = central angle in degrees
    • r = radius of the circle
    • πr² = area of the whole circle
  • Alternative form (proportion):
    • Sector Area / Circle Area = θ / 360°
    • This shows: sector is to whole circle as angle is to 360°
  • Key pattern to recognize:
    • Arc length uses circumference (2πr)
    • Sector area uses circle area (πr²)
    • Both use the same proportion: (θ/360°)

3. Solving Arc and Sector Problems

  • Step-by-step approach for arc length:
    • Step 1: Identify the central angle (θ) and radius (r)
    • Step 2: Calculate the full circumference: 2πr
    • Step 3: Find the fraction: θ/360°
    • Step 4: Multiply: arc length = (θ/360°) × 2πr
  • Step-by-step approach for sector area:
    • Step 1: Identify the central angle (θ) and radius (r)
    • Step 2: Calculate the full circle area: πr²
    • Step 3: Find the fraction: θ/360°
    • Step 4: Multiply: sector area = (θ/360°) × πr²
  • Working backwards (finding angle or radius):
    • If given arc length and radius, solve for θ
    • If given sector area and angle, solve for r
    • Use algebra to isolate the unknown variable
  • Common question types:
    • Given angle and radius, find arc length or sector area
    • Given arc length and radius, find the central angle
    • Given sector area and angle, find the radius
    • Finding the difference between two arcs or sectors

4. Test-Taking Tips for Arcs and Sectors

  • Think in terms of proportions
    • The key insight: the arc/sector is a fraction of the whole circle
    • That fraction equals the central angle divided by 360°
    • Example: A 90° angle means 90/360 = 1/4 of the circle
  • Don't confuse arc length with sector area
    • Arc length is distance (1-dimensional) - uses circumference
    • Sector area is space (2-dimensional) - uses circle area
    • Read the question carefully to determine which one is asked
  • Memorize the formulas now
    • Arc length = (θ/360°) × 2πr
    • Sector area = (θ/360°) × πr²
    • These are NOT provided on the test
  • Use the proportion setup for complex problems
    • Arc/Circumference = Angle/360° is easier to manipulate algebraically
    • Especially useful when solving for the angle or radius
    • Cross-multiply to solve
  • Simplify the fraction θ/360° early
    • 90/360 = 1/4, 120/360 = 1/3, 180/360 = 1/2
    • This makes calculations much simpler
    • Example: (90/360) × 2π(12) = (1/4) × 24π = 6π

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Arc length = (θ/360°) × 2πr and Sector area = (θ/360°) × πr²—these formulas are NOT provided on the ACT
  • ✓The central angle determines what fraction of the circle you have: a 90° angle = 1/4 of the circle
  • ✓Arc length is 1-dimensional (uses circumference 2πr), sector area is 2-dimensional (uses circle area πr²)
  • ✓Think proportionally: arc/circumference = sector/circle = angle/360°—this relationship solves all arc and sector problems
