Core algebra skills appear on almost every ACT Math question, from simple equation solving to complex multi-step problems. Mastering PEMDAS, negative numbers, and combining like terms is essential for avoiding careless mistakes and solving problems quickly.

1. PEMDAS (Order of Operations)

  • PEMDAS is the order you MUST follow when solving expressions:
    • P - Parentheses: Complete calculations inside parentheses first
    • E - Exponents: Next, complete any exponents or square roots
    • MD - Multiplication and Division: Work LEFT TO RIGHT
    • AS - Addition and Subtraction: Work LEFT TO RIGHT
  • CRITICAL: Work left-to-right for MD and AS
    • This is where the ACT tricks students most often!
    • Example: 16 ÷ 4 × 2 - 3
    • ✓ Correct: 16 ÷ 4 × 2 - 3 = 4 × 2 - 3 = 8 - 3 = 5
    • ✗ Wrong: Don't do 4 × 2 first! That gives 16 ÷ 8 - 3 = -1 (incorrect)
  • Calculator shortcut:
    • Your calculator is programmed to follow PEMDAS automatically
    • Enter the expression exactly as written and let the calculator solve it
    • This prevents order of operations mistakes
    • Make sure to use parentheses when needed!
  • Common PEMDAS mistakes to avoid:
    • Doing multiplication before division (or vice versa) instead of left-to-right
    • Forgetting to complete parentheses first
    • Doing exponents after multiplication/division

2. Working with Negative Numbers

  • The ACT LOVES negative number questions—these are prime mistake opportunities
    • Students make more careless errors with negatives than any other topic
    • Take extra care and write out every step
  • Subtracting negative numbers:
    • Subtracting a negative = adding a positive
    • Example: 2 - (-4) = 2 + 4 = 6
    • Think: "minus a minus is a plus"
  • Distributing negative signs (HUGE mistake area):
    • You MUST distribute the negative to ALL terms in parentheses
    • Example: 5x - (2x - 5)
    • ✓ Correct: 5x - 2x - (-5) = 5x - 2x + 5 = 3x + 5
    • ✗ Wrong: 5x - 2x - 5 = 3x - 5 (forgot to distribute to the -5!)
    • The negative sign affects BOTH the 2x AND the -5
  • Negative numbers and exponents (another huge trap):
    • -x² is NOT the same as (-x)²
    • -x²: Square x first, then make it negative → -x² = -(x²)
    • (-x)²: Square the entire negative x → (-x)² = (-x)(-x) = x²
    • Example: -2² = -(2²) = -4
    • Example: (-2)² = (-2)(-2) = 4
    • Calculator tip: ALWAYS use parentheses around negative numbers!

3. Combining Like Terms

  • Like terms have the same variable raised to the same power:
    • 3x and 5x are like terms → can be combined to 8x
    • 2x² and 4x² are like terms → can be combined to 6x²
    • 3x and 5x² are NOT like terms → cannot be combined
    • Constants (numbers without variables) can always be combined
  • Steps for combining like terms:
    • Step 1: Distribute any parentheses
    • Step 2: Group like terms together
    • Step 3: Add or subtract the coefficients
    • Step 4: Simplify the expression
  • Example: 2(3x - 6) + 3y = 6x - y + 8, solve for y:
    • Distribute: 6x - 12 + 3y = 6x - y + 8
    • The 6x terms cancel on both sides
    • Simplify: -12 + 3y = -y + 8
    • Combine: 4y = 20
    • Solve: y = 5
  • When to combine like terms:
    • Always simplify equations before solving
    • Combining like terms reduces the equation to its simplest form
    • This makes it easier to see what to do next

4. Cross-Multiplying Fractions

  • When two fractions are equal, you can cross-multiply:
    • If x/y = z/w, then xw = yz
    • Multiply the numerator of each fraction by the denominator of the other
    • This eliminates the fractions and gives you a simpler equation
  • Visual representation:
    • x/y = z/w
    • Cross-multiply diagonally: x × w and y × z
    • Result: xw = yz
  • Example: If x/4 = 6/7, solve for x:
    • Cross-multiply: 7x = 4(6)
    • Simplify: 7x = 24
    • Solve: x = 24/7
  • When to use cross-multiplication:
    • When you have one fraction equal to another fraction
    • This is the fastest way to eliminate fractions
    • Much faster than finding common denominators

5. Test-Taking Tips for Algebra Skills

  • Write out every step—do NOT do mental math
    • Careless errors happen when you skip steps
    • Writing forces you to be careful with negative signs
    • You can check your work if you write it out
  • Use your calculator strategically
    • For PEMDAS problems: enter the entire expression
    • ALWAYS use parentheses around negative numbers
    • Double-check your calculator entry matches the problem
  • Circle or underline negative signs
    • Makes them more visible so you don't forget them
    • Especially important when distributing
  • Test your answer with substitution
    • If you solved for x, plug your answer back into the original equation
    • Does it make the equation true? Then you're correct!
    • This catches algebra mistakes
  • Master the basics before moving to advanced topics
    • These fundamental skills appear in EVERY algebra problem
    • You cannot do advanced algebra without mastering these basics
    • Practice until negative numbers and PEMDAS are automatic

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Follow PEMDAS strictly, working LEFT TO RIGHT for multiplication/division and addition/subtraction—this is where most mistakes happen
  • ✓When distributing a negative sign, apply it to ALL terms: 5x - (2x - 5) = 5x - 2x + 5, not 5x - 2x - 5
  • ✓-x² ≠ (-x)²: without parentheses, square first then negate (-x² = -4 when x=2), with parentheses square the negative ((-x)² = 4)
  • ✓Write out every step and always use parentheses in your calculator for negative numbers—careless mistakes lose more points than not knowing the math
