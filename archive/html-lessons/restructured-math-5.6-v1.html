Repeating patterns appear frequently on the ACT, testing your ability to find specific terms in a repeating decimal, the units digit of large powers, or values of powers of i. The key is identifying the pattern length and finding the nearest multiple.

1. Repeating Decimals

  • Understanding repeating decimals:
    • A line over digits shows they repeat forever
    • Example: 0.1234̄ = 0.1234123412341234...
    • The repeating unit is 1234 (4 digits long)
    • Pattern repeats endlessly without stopping
  • Example: Find the 307th decimal place of 0.34562̄
    • Repeating unit: 34562 (5 digits)
    • Pattern: 3, 4, 5, 6, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 2...
    • Every 5th position is a "2" (5th, 10th, 15th, 20th, ...)
    • Find multiple of 5 near 307: closest is 305
    • 305th position = 2
    • 306th = 3, 307th = 4
  • Another example: Find 703rd place of 0.596127̄
    • Repeating unit: 596127 (6 digits)
    • Every 6th position is a "7" (6th, 12th, 18th, ...)
    • Find multiple of 6 near 703: closest is 702
    • 702nd position = 7
    • 703rd = 5 (first digit in pattern)

2. Units Digit of Powers

  • The units digit is the ones place:
    • Example: units digit of 945 is 5
    • Example: units digit of 10,381 is 1
    • For very large powers, use the repeating pattern
  • Example: Find units digit of 7^122
    • Write out powers of 7 and observe units digits:
    • 7^1 = 7, 7^2 = 49, 7^3 = 343, 7^4 = 2,401
    • 7^5 = 16,807, 7^6 = 117,649, 7^7 = 823,543, 7^8 = 5,764,801
    • Units digits: 7, 9, 3, 1, 7, 9, 3, 1...
    • Pattern repeats every 4 terms
    • Every 4th power has units digit 1 (4th, 8th, 12th, ...)
    • Find multiple of 4 near 122: closest is 120
    • 7^120 has units digit 1
    • 7^121 has units digit 7, 7^122 has units digit 9
  • CRITICAL: Memorize common patterns (repeat every 4 powers):
    • Powers of 2: units digits are 2, 4, 8, 6, 2, 4, 8, 6...
    • Powers of 3: units digits are 3, 9, 7, 1, 3, 9, 7, 1...
    • Powers of 7: units digits are 7, 9, 3, 1, 7, 9, 3, 1...
    • Powers of 8: units digits are 8, 4, 2, 6, 8, 4, 2, 6...
    • All patterns repeat every 4 terms!

3. Algebraic Repeating Pattern Problems

  • When given a variable exponent:
    • Use the pattern to determine the value
    • Pick a specific value that matches the condition
    • Count forward or backward in the pattern
  • Example: 3^x has a 9 in the ones place. What digit does 3^(x+3) have?
    • Pattern for powers of 3: 3, 9, 7, 1, 3, 9, 7, 1...
    • If 3^x ends in 9, then x could be 2, 6, 10, 14, etc.
    • Pick x = 2 (since 3^2 = 9)
    • Then 3^(x+3) = 3^5 = 243
    • Units digit is 3
    • OR: Starting from 9, count 3 forward: 9 → 7 → 1 → 3
  • Key insight:
    • Any value of x that gives the same units digit works
    • Counting forward/backward in pattern gives consistent result
    • Pick the simplest value to make calculations easier

4. Powers of i (Complex Numbers)

  • Definition: i = √(-1)
    • i^2 = -1 (by definition)
    • Powers of i follow a repeating pattern
    • Pattern repeats every 4 terms
  • CRITICAL pattern to memorize:
    • i^1 = i
    • i^2 = -1
    • i^3 = -i
    • i^4 = 1
    • i^5 = i, i^6 = -1, i^7 = -i, i^8 = 1...
    • Pattern: i, -1, -i, 1, i, -1, -i, 1...
  • Example: Simplify 10i^47 - 7
    • Pattern repeats every 4 terms
    • Every 4th power equals 1 (i^4, i^8, i^12, ...)
    • Find multiple of 4 near 47: closest is 44 or 48
    • Using 48: i^48 = 1
    • Count backward: i^47 = -i
    • Substitute: 10(-i) - 7 = -10i - 7
  • Example: If k is a multiple of 4, find i^(4k+1)
    • Pick k = 4 (any multiple of 4 works)
    • i^(4k+1) = i^(4·4+1) = i^17
    • Find multiple of 4 near 17: i^16 = 1
    • Count forward: i^17 = i

5. General Strategy for Repeating Patterns

  • Step-by-step process:
    • Step 1: Identify the repeating unit and its length
    • Step 2: Go to the END of the repeating unit
    • Step 3: Find the nearest multiple of the pattern length
    • Step 4: Count forward or backward to find the target term
  • Example workflow for 0.235638̄, finding 103rd digit:
    • Step 1: Repeating unit is 235638 (6 digits)
    • Step 2: End of unit is "8"
    • Step 3: Multiple of 6 near 103 is 102
    • Step 4: 102nd digit = 8, so 103rd digit = 2
  • Tips for success:
    • For repeating decimals: count the digits under the bar
    • For powers: most patterns repeat every 4 terms
    • For powers of i: pattern is always every 4 terms
    • If pattern isn't memorized, write out first several terms
    • Use division to find nearest multiple quickly
  • Other applications:
    • Days of the week (pattern repeats every 7 days)
    • Calendar problems (months, seasons)
    • Cyclical sequences in geometry or algebra
