Unit conversion appears frequently on the ACT, testing your ability to convert between feet and inches, yards and feet, and various rates. The key skill is dimensional analysis, where conversion factors are set up to cancel units.

1. Basic Unit Conversions

  • Memorize these standard conversions:
    • 1 foot = 12 inches
    • 1 yard = 3 feet
    • 1 hour = 60 minutes
    • 1 minute = 60 seconds
    • For less common conversions (pounds to kilograms, etc.), the ACT provides the conversion factor
  • Converting simple lengths:
    • Example: Convert 10 feet to inches
    • 10 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot) = 120 inches
    • The "feet" units cancel, leaving inches
  • Mixed units (feet and inches):
    • Example: 10 feet 3 inches = ?
    • Step 1: Convert feet to inches: 10 × 12 = 120 inches
    • Step 2: Add the extra inches: 120 + 3 = 123 inches
    • Step 3: To convert back: 123 ÷ 12 = 10 remainder 3 → 10 feet 3 inches

2. Area and Volume Unit Conversions

  • CRITICAL rule for area/volume conversions:
    • Convert the units BEFORE calculating area or volume
    • Never calculate area/volume first and then try to convert
    • This is a common trap on the ACT!
  • Example: Tennis court is 78 feet long and 36 feet wide. Find area in square yards.
    • WRONG: Calculate 78 × 36 = 2,808 square feet, then convert
    • RIGHT: Convert dimensions first
    • Length: 78 feet × (1 yard / 3 feet) = 26 yards
    • Width: 36 feet × (1 yard / 3 feet) = 12 yards
    • Area: 26 × 12 = 312 square yards
  • Pay attention to bolded units:
    • When the ACT bolds units in the question, it's a warning
    • Double-check you're solving for the correct units
    • This prevents accidentally giving an answer in wrong units

3. Dimensional Analysis (Advanced Conversions)

  • Dimensional analysis uses conversion factors to convert between units:
    • Step 1: Start with the given value
    • Step 2: Multiply by conversion factors to switch units
    • Step 3: Set up fractions so units cancel correctly
    • Step 4: Multiply and divide to get final answer
  • Example: Convert 30 meters per second to kilometers per hour (1 km = 1,000 m)
    • Set up as fraction: 30 m / 1 s
    • Convert meters to kilometers: (30 m / 1 s) × (1 km / 1,000 m) = 30 km / 1,000 s
    • Convert seconds to minutes: (30 km / 1,000 s) × (60 s / 1 min) = 1,800 km / 1,000 min
    • Convert minutes to hours: (1,800 km / 1,000 min) × (60 min / 1 hr) = 108,000 km / 1,000 hr
    • Simplify: 108,000 ÷ 1,000 = 108 km/hr
  • One-step method (all at once):
    • (30 m / 1 s) × (1 km / 1,000 m) × (60 s / 1 min) × (60 min / 1 hr)
    • = (30 × 1 × 60 × 60) / (1 × 1,000 × 1 × 1) = 108,000 / 1,000 = 108 km/hr
    • All units except km and hr cancel out
  • Key principle:
    • If units cancel correctly, your setup is right!
    • Unwanted units should appear once in numerator and once in denominator
    • Desired units should remain after cancellation

4. Rate Problems with Unit Conversion

  • Distance = Speed × Time (must have matching units):
    • If speed is in miles per hour, time must be in hours
    • If speed is in miles per minute, time must be in minutes
    • Convert one or both to make units match
  • Example: Erica bikes 24 mph for 40 minutes. How far does she travel?
    • Problem: Speed is per hour, time is in minutes (units don't match!)
    • Solution: Convert speed to miles per minute
    • 24 miles/hr × (1 hr / 60 min) = 0.4 miles/min
    • Distance = 0.4 miles/min × 40 min = 16 miles
  • Time = Distance ÷ Speed:
    • Example: How long to travel 16 miles at 18 mph?
    • Convert speed: 18 mph × (1 hr / 60 min) = 0.3 miles/min
    • Time = 16 miles ÷ 0.3 miles/min = 53.3 minutes
  • Volume conversions (gallons to quarts):
    • Example: Two 50-gallon bags, using 3 quarts per loaf (1 gallon = 4 quarts)
    • Total flour: 2 × 50 = 100 gallons
    • Convert: 100 gallons × (4 quarts / 1 gallon) = 400 quarts
    • Loaves: 400 quarts ÷ 3 quarts/loaf = 133.3 loaves

5. Algebraic Unit Conversion

  • When variables are involved:
    • Same process as numeric conversions
    • Use conversion factors with variables
    • Simplify algebraically
  • Example: A pipe p feet long is cut into n pieces. Each piece is x inches. Find x.
    • Step 1: Convert feet to inches
    • p feet × (12 inches / 1 foot) = 12p inches
    • Step 2: Divide by number of pieces
    • x = 12p / n inches per piece
  • Alternative: Substitution method
    • Pick easy numbers: Let p = 2 feet, n = 3 pieces
    • Convert: 2 feet × 12 = 24 inches
    • Divide: 24 ÷ 3 = 8 inches per piece, so x = 8
    • Plug p = 2, n = 3, x = 8 into answer choices to find which works
    • This is faster when algebra seems complicated!
  • Common algebraic conversions:
    • f feet = 12f inches
    • y yards = 3y feet = 36y inches
    • h hours = 60h minutes = 3,600h seconds
