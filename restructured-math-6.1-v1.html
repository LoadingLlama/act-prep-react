Mean, median, mode, and range are fundamental statistical measures. The ACT most frequently tests the mean (average), requiring you to find missing values or combine averages from different groups.

1. Basic Definitions

  • Four statistical measures you must know:
    • Mean (average): The sum divided by the number of items
    • Median: The middle number when ordered from smallest to largest
    • Mode: The number that appears most often
    • Range: The difference between the largest and smallest numbers
  • The fundamental average formula:
    • Average = Sum ÷ Number of Items
    • Rearrange to: Sum = Average × Number of Items
    • This rearranged form is crucial for many ACT questions!
    • Memorize both forms
  • Example: Find the mean of {2, 5, 8, 9}
    • Sum = 2 + 5 + 8 + 9 = 24
    • Number of items = 4
    • Mean = 24 ÷ 4 = 6

2. Finding Missing Values Using Mean

  • The most common ACT question type:
    • You're given the average and most values
    • Asked to find a missing value
    • Use algebra to solve
  • Example: Five students averaged 24 points. Four scored 22, 28, 17, and 25. Find the fifth score.
    • Step 1: Set up the equation
    • Let x = fifth student's score
    • 24 = (22 + 28 + 17 + 25 + x) ÷ 5
    • Step 2: Simplify
    • 24 = (92 + x) ÷ 5
    • Step 3: Multiply both sides by 5
    • 120 = 92 + x
    • Step 4: Solve for x
    • x = 28
  • CRITICAL shortcut:
    • Always multiply average by number of items first
    • This gives you the total sum immediately
    • Makes solving much easier!
    • In example above: 24 × 5 = 120 (total sum)

3. Combining Averages

  • CRITICAL: You CANNOT just average the averages!
    • When combining groups with different sizes
    • Must find total sum for ALL items
    • Then divide by total number of items
  • Example: Period 1 has 6 students averaging 85. Period 2 has 9 students averaging 90. Find combined average.
    • Step 1: Find total points for Period 1
    • Sum = Average × Number
    • Sum₁ = 85 × 6 = 510 points
    • Step 2: Find total points for Period 2
    • Sum₂ = 90 × 9 = 810 points
    • Step 3: Calculate combined average
    • Combined Average = (510 + 810) ÷ (6 + 9)
    • Combined Average = 1320 ÷ 15 = 88
  • Common mistake to avoid:
    • WRONG: (85 + 90) ÷ 2 = 87.5
    • This ignores that groups have different sizes
    • Period 2 has MORE students, so its average should count more
    • Always convert to total sums first!

4. Working with Median

  • The median is positional:
    • Depends on WHERE values fall in ordered list
    • NOT the numerical values themselves
    • ALWAYS order the data first!
  • Finding median with odd number of values:
    • Example: {4, 1, 9, 3, 7}
    • Step 1: Order: {1, 3, 4, 7, 9}
    • Step 2: Pick middle value: 4
    • Median = 4
  • Finding median with even number of values:
    • Example: {2, 8, 5, 9, 3, 6}
    • Step 1: Order: {2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9}
    • Step 2: Find two middle values: 5 and 6
    • Step 3: Average them: (5 + 6) ÷ 2 = 5.5
    • Median = 5.5
  • Finding missing value when median is given:
    • Example: Median of {3, 7, x, 12} is 8. Find x.
    • Step 1: With 4 values, median = average of 2nd and 3rd
    • Step 2: Order known values: {3, ?, ?, 12}
    • Step 3: Test where x fits
    • If x ≥ 7, order is {3, 7, x, 12}
    • Median = (7 + x) ÷ 2 = 8
    • 7 + x = 16 → x = 9 ✓

5. Mode and Range

  • Finding the mode:
    • Count how many times each value appears
    • Mode = value(s) with highest frequency
    • Example: {3, 5, 5, 7, 7, 7, 9} → Mode = 7 (appears 3 times)
    • Can have multiple modes if values tie for most frequent
    • No mode if all values appear equally
  • Finding the range:
    • Range = Maximum - Minimum
    • Example: {12, 4, 18, 7, 9}
    • Max = 18, Min = 4
    • Range = 18 - 4 = 14
  • How adding values affects statistics:
    • Adding value equal to current mean → mean stays same
    • Adding value larger than max → range increases
    • Adding value smaller than min → range increases
    • Adding value between min and max → range stays same

Key Takeaways

  • ✓The fundamental formula: Average = Sum ÷ Number of Items (rearrange to: Sum = Average × Number)
  • ✓When combining averages, find each group's total sum first (Sum = Average × Count), add sums, then divide by total count—NEVER just average the averages!
  • ✓For median, ALWAYS order data first: odd count = pick middle value; even count = average two middle values
  • ✓Mode = most frequent value; Range = Max - Min
