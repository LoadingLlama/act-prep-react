Ratios and proportions compare quantities and solve for unknown values using equivalent relationships.

1. Ratios

Ratio a:b means a/b

Example: 3:5 means 3/5 or 3 to 5

2. Proportions

Set two ratios equal and cross-multiply to solve:

  • If a/b = c/d, then ad = bc
  • Example: 3/5 = x/20 → 3(20) = 5x → x = 12

3. Part-to-Whole

If ratio of boys to girls is 3:5, and there are 24 total students:

  • Total parts: 3 + 5 = 8
  • Boys: (3/8) × 24 = 9
  • Girls: (5/8) × 24 = 15

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Ratios compare quantities: a:b = a/b
  • ✓Cross-multiply proportions: a/b = c/d → ad = bc
  • ✓Part-to-whole: use total parts to find individual amounts
