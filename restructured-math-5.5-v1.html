Scientific notation expresses very large or very small numbers in a compact form. The ACT tests converting to and from scientific notation, as well as operations with numbers in this form.

1. Scientific Notation Basics

  • Format of scientific notation:
    • Always written as: (digit term) × 10^(power)
    • Example: 15,000,000 = 1.5 × 10^7
    • Digit term: 1.5 (must be ≥ 1 and < 10)
    • Exponential term: 10^7 (base is always 10)
  • CRITICAL requirement for digit term:
    • Digit term must be: 1 ≤ digit term < 10
    • Example: 2.5 × 10^4 is CORRECT
    • Example: 25 × 10^3 is WRONG (digit term too large)
    • Example: 0.25 × 10^5 is WRONG (digit term too small)
  • Why use scientific notation:
    • Makes very large numbers easier to read and write
    • Makes very small numbers easier to express
    • Common in science for astronomical or microscopic values

2. Converting Large Numbers to Scientific Notation

  • Process for numbers greater than 10:
    • Step 1: Place decimal after the first non-zero digit
    • Step 2: Count how many places the decimal moved LEFT
    • Step 3: That count is the POSITIVE exponent
  • Example: Convert 15,000,000 to scientific notation
    • Original: 15,000,000. (decimal at end)
    • Move decimal between 1 and 5: 1.5
    • Count moves: 7 places to the LEFT
    • Result: 1.5 × 10^7
  • More examples:
    • 201,000 → decimal moves 5 left → 2.01 × 10^5
    • 8,320 → decimal moves 3 left → 8.32 × 10^3
    • 250,000 → decimal moves 5 left → 2.5 × 10^5
  • Rule to remember:
    • Moving decimal LEFT → POSITIVE exponent
    • Large numbers → positive powers of 10

3. Converting Small Numbers to Scientific Notation

  • Process for numbers less than 1:
    • Step 1: Move decimal RIGHT until after first non-zero digit
    • Step 2: Count how many places the decimal moved RIGHT
    • Step 3: That count is the NEGATIVE exponent
  • Example: Convert 0.000916 to scientific notation
    • Original: 0.000916
    • Move decimal after the 9: 9.16
    • Count moves: 4 places to the RIGHT
    • Result: 9.16 × 10^(-4)
  • More examples:
    • 0.0000005 → decimal moves 7 right → 5 × 10^(-7)
    • 0.000036 → decimal moves 5 right → 3.6 × 10^(-5)
    • 0.0000029 → decimal moves 6 right → 2.9 × 10^(-6)
  • Rule to remember:
    • Moving decimal RIGHT → NEGATIVE exponent
    • Small numbers (< 1) → negative powers of 10
    • Digit term still must be between 1 and 10

4. Operations with Scientific Notation

  • Multiplication:
    • Multiply digit terms, add exponents
    • Example: (2 × 10^3) × (3 × 10^5)
    • = (2 × 3) × 10^(3+5)
    • = 6 × 10^8
  • Division:
    • Divide digit terms, subtract exponents
    • Example: (8 × 10^7) ÷ (2 × 10^3)
    • = (8 ÷ 2) × 10^(7-3)
    • = 4 × 10^4
  • Addition/Subtraction (same exponent):
    • Only possible when exponents are the SAME
    • Add or subtract digit terms, keep exponent
    • Example: (3.5 × 10^6) + (2.1 × 10^6) = 5.6 × 10^6
    • Example: (9.7 × 10^4) - (4.3 × 10^4) = 5.4 × 10^4
  • Addition/Subtraction (different exponents):
    • Must convert to same exponent first
    • Or convert both to standard form, add/subtract, convert back
    • Example: (2.6 × 10^6) + (7.8 × 10^5)
    • Convert: 2.6 × 10^6 = 26 × 10^5
    • Add: (26 × 10^5) + (7.8 × 10^5) = 33.8 × 10^5
    • Adjust: 33.8 × 10^5 = 3.38 × 10^6

5. Word Problems and Applications

  • Real-world applications:
    • Population calculations (millions or billions)
    • Distance measurements (astronomical units)
    • Microscopic measurements (atoms, cells)
    • Financial calculations (national debt, budgets)
  • Percentage problems with scientific notation:
    • Example: What is 8% of 9.02 × 10^4?
    • Step 1: Convert percentage: 8% = 0.08
    • Step 2: Multiply: 0.08 × 9.02 × 10^4
    • Step 3: 0.08 × 9.02 = 0.7216
    • Step 4: 0.7216 × 10^4 = 7,216
  • Ratio problems:
    • Example: Area of Canada (9.89 × 10^6) to Connecticut (1.44 × 10^4)
    • Ratio = 9.89 × 10^6 / 1.44 × 10^4
    • = (9.89 / 1.44) × 10^(6-4)
    • ≈ 6.87 × 10^2 = 687:1
  • Solving for unknown exponents:
    • Example: (3.75 × 10^(3+a)) × (7 × 10^2) = 26,250
    • Step 1: Multiply: 3.75 × 7 = 26.25
    • Step 2: Exponents: 10^(3+a+2) = 10^(5+a)
    • Step 3: So 26.25 × 10^(5+a) = 26,250
    • Step 4: Convert 26,250 = 2.625 × 10^4
    • Step 5: Also 26.25 × 10^(5+a) = 2.625 × 10^(6+a)
    • Step 6: Match exponents: 6 + a = 4 → a = -2

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Digit term must satisfy 1 ≤ digit < 10; anything else is incorrect scientific notation
  • ✓Large numbers: move decimal LEFT → positive exponent; small numbers: move decimal RIGHT → negative exponent
  • ✓Multiply: multiply digits, add exponents; Divide: divide digits, subtract exponents
  • ✓Add/Subtract: exponents must match first; convert to same exponent or use standard form
