Arcs and sectors are parts of circles that appear frequently on the ACT. An arc is a piece of the circle's edge (circumference), and a sector is a "slice" of the circle—like a pizza slice. Once you understand how to work with fractions of a circle, these problems become easy.

1. Circle Fundamentals Review

Before working with arcs and sectors, let's review the essential circle parts and formulas.

  • Radius (r): Distance from center to edge
  • Diameter (d): Distance across through center = 2r
  • Circumference (C): Distance around the circle = 2πr = πd
  • Area (A): Space inside the circle = πr²
  • Arc: Part of the circumference
  • Sector: "Pizza slice" part of the area

2. The Master Concept: Fractions of a Circle

A full circle has 360°. If your central angle is θ degrees, then you have θ/360 of the whole circle.

The Master Formula

  • Fraction of circle = θ/360
  • If θ = 90°, you have 90/360 = 1/4 of the circle
  • If θ = 180°, you have 180/360 = 1/2 of the circle
  • If θ = 120°, you have 120/360 = 1/3 of the circle

Quick Trick: Memorize Common Angles

  • 90° = 1/4 of circle
  • 120° = 1/3 of circle
  • 180° = 1/2 of circle
  • 270° = 3/4 of circle

3. Arc Length Formula

Arc length is a fraction of the circumference.

Step 1: Identify the Formula

  • Arc Length = (θ/360) × 2πr
  • In words: Take the fraction of the circle, multiply by the full circumference

Step 2: Plug in Known Values

  • Identify θ (the central angle in degrees)
  • Identify r (the radius)
  • Substitute into the formula

Step 3: Simplify

  • Reduce the fraction θ/360 first
  • Multiply by 2πr
  • Leave answer in terms of π unless asked to approximate

Example 1: Arc Length

Problem: A circle has radius 10. What is the length of a 90° arc?

Solution:

  • Arc Length = (θ/360) × 2πr
  • Arc Length = (90/360) × 2π(10)
  • Arc Length = (1/4) × 20π
  • Arc Length = 5π

4. Sector Area Formula

Sector area is a fraction of the total area.

Step 1: Identify the Formula

  • Sector Area = (θ/360) × πr²
  • In words: Take the fraction of the circle, multiply by the full area

Step 2: Plug in Known Values

  • Identify θ (the central angle in degrees)
  • Identify r (the radius)
  • Substitute into the formula

Step 3: Simplify

  • Reduce the fraction θ/360 first
  • Square the radius (r²)
  • Multiply by π and the fraction

Example 2: Sector Area

Problem: A circle has radius 6. What is the area of a 120° sector?

Solution:

  • Sector Area = (θ/360) × πr²
  • Sector Area = (120/360) × π(6²)
  • Sector Area = (1/3) × 36π
  • Sector Area = 12π

Example 3: Finding the Angle

Problem: A sector with radius 8 has an area of 16π. What is the central angle?

Solution:

  • Sector Area = (θ/360) × πr²
  • 16π = (θ/360) × π(8²)
  • 16π = (θ/360) × 64π
  • 16 = (θ/360) × 64
  • 16/64 = θ/360
  • 1/4 = θ/360
  • θ = 90°

6. When to Use These Formulas

  • Use arc length when:
    • Problem asks about distance along the edge
    • Finding perimeter of a shape that includes a curved edge
    • Calculating how far something travels along a circular path
  • Use sector area when:
    • Problem asks about the "slice" of a circle
    • Finding area of regions bounded by two radii and an arc
    • Calculating part of a circular region
  • Remember:
    • Arc length deals with the edge (1-dimensional)
    • Sector area deals with the region (2-dimensional)
