Function transformations allow you to predict how graphs shift, stretch, reflect, and compress based on changes to the equation. Understanding vertical shifts, horizontal shifts, reflections, and stretches/compressions helps you quickly sketch graphs and solve ACT problems involving function families.

1. What Are Function Transformations?

Function transformations are systematic changes to a function's equation that produce predictable changes to its graph. Instead of plotting points, you can transform a parent function to create new graphs.

  • Key advantages:
    • Faster than plotting points to graph functions
    • Helps visualize how equations relate to graphs
    • Essential for understanding function families (quadratics, absolute value, etc.)
    • Frequently tested on ACT Math section
  • Four main types of transformations:
    • Vertical shifts: Move graph up or down
    • Horizontal shifts: Move graph left or right
    • Reflections: Flip graph over an axis
    • Stretches and compressions: Make graph taller/shorter or wider/narrower

2. Types of Transformations

Vertical Shifts: f(x) + k

  • Vertical shifts move the entire graph up or down without changing its shape
    • f(x) + k: shifts UP k units when k > 0
    • f(x) - k: shifts DOWN k units when k > 0
    • Every point (x, y) becomes (x, y + k)
    • Example: y = x² + 3 shifts the parabola y = x² up 3 units

Horizontal Shifts: f(x - h)

  • Horizontal shifts move the entire graph left or right (opposite of what you might expect!)
    • f(x - h): shifts RIGHT h units when h > 0
    • f(x + h): shifts LEFT h units when h > 0
    • Every point (x, y) becomes (x + h, y)
    • Example: y = (x - 2)² shifts the parabola y = x² right 2 units
    • Common mistake: The sign is opposite of the direction!

Vertical Stretch and Compression: a · f(x)

  • Multiplying the function by a constant stretches or compresses vertically
    • a · f(x) where |a| > 1: STRETCH (graph becomes taller)
    • a · f(x) where 0 < |a| < 1: COMPRESSION (graph becomes shorter)
    • Every point (x, y) becomes (x, a·y)
    • Example: y = 3x² stretches y = x² vertically by factor of 3
    • Example: y = (1/2)x² compresses y = x² vertically by factor of 1/2

Horizontal Stretch and Compression: f(b · x)

  • Multiplying x by a constant stretches or compresses horizontally (opposite effect!)
    • f(b·x) where |b| > 1: COMPRESSION (graph becomes narrower)
    • f(b·x) where 0 < |b| < 1: STRETCH (graph becomes wider)
    • Every point (x, y) becomes (x/b, y)
    • Example: y = (2x)² compresses y = x² horizontally by factor of 1/2

Reflections: -f(x) and f(-x)

  • Reflections flip the graph over an axis
    • -f(x): reflects over x-axis (flips vertically)
    • f(-x): reflects over y-axis (flips horizontally)
    • Example: y = -x² reflects y = x² over x-axis (opens downward)
    • Example: y = |−x| reflects y = |x| over y-axis (though |x| looks the same)

3. Order of Transformations

When multiple transformations appear in one function, apply them in this order:

Step 1: Horizontal Transformations (inside the function)

  • Apply transformations that affect x first
    • Horizontal shifts: f(x - h) or f(x + h)
    • Horizontal stretches/compressions: f(b·x)
    • Reflection over y-axis: f(-x)

Step 2: Vertical Transformations (outside the function)

  • Apply transformations that affect y (the output) second
    • Vertical stretches/compressions: a · f(x)
    • Reflection over x-axis: -f(x)
    • Vertical shifts: f(x) + k or f(x) - k

4. Examples

Example 1: Combined Transformations

Describe how the graph of g(x) = -2(x - 3)² + 4 is related to the parent function f(x) = x².

Solution:

  • Start with f(x) = x² (parent parabola)
  • Horizontal shift right 3: (x - 3)²
  • Vertical stretch by factor of 2: 2(x - 3)²
  • Reflect over x-axis: -2(x - 3)²
  • Vertical shift up 4: -2(x - 3)² + 4

Result: The parabola shifts right 3, stretches vertically by 2, flips upside down, and moves up 4 units.

Example 2: Identifying Transformations from Equations

If f(x) = |x|, which transformation produces g(x) = |x + 2| - 5?

Solution:

  • Compare g(x) = |x + 2| - 5 to f(x) = |x|
  • The (x + 2) means shift LEFT 2 units (opposite of the sign!)
  • The -5 means shift DOWN 5 units
  • Combined: Shift left 2 and down 5

5. When to Use Transformations

Look for transformation questions when you see:

  • Parent functions like f(x) = x², f(x) = |x|, f(x) = √x, or f(x) = 1/x
  • Questions asking "how does the graph of g(x) relate to f(x)"
  • Equations with constants added/subtracted or multiplied inside or outside the function
  • Questions about graph shifts, stretches, or reflections

6. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Horizontal shifts are opposite the sign: f(x - 2) shifts RIGHT, not left
  • Vertical shifts match the sign: f(x) + 2 shifts UP
  • Horizontal stretch/compression is opposite intuition: f(2x) compresses (makes narrower)
  • Remember to apply horizontal transformations before vertical ones
  • Don't confuse -f(x) (reflection over x-axis) with f(-x) (reflection over y-axis)

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Vertical shifts (f(x) + k) move graphs up/down and match the sign
  • ✓Horizontal shifts (f(x - h)) move graphs right/left and are opposite the sign
  • ✓Vertical stretch/compression (a·f(x)): |a| > 1 stretches, 0 < |a| < 1 compresses
  • ✓Reflections: -f(x) flips over x-axis, f(-x) flips over y-axis
  • ✓Apply horizontal transformations (inside) before vertical transformations (outside)
