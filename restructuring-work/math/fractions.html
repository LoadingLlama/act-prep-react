Working with fractions efficiently requires mastering arithmetic operations, simplification techniques, and algebraic fraction manipulation. These skills appear in approximately 15% of ACT Math questions and are essential for rational expressions, proportion problems, and complex calculations.

1. Simplifying and Comparing Fractions

Reducing Fractions to Lowest Terms

To simplify a fraction, divide both numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (GCF).

Example: Simplify 24/36.

GCF of 24 and 36 is 12
24 ÷ 12 = 2
36 ÷ 12 = 3
Result: 2/3

Example: Simplify 45/75.

GCF is 15
45 ÷ 15 = 3
75 ÷ 15 = 5
Result: 3/5

Converting Between Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions

Mixed number to improper fraction: Multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator, place over the original denominator.

Improper fraction to mixed number: Divide numerator by denominator; quotient is the whole number, remainder is the new numerator.

Example: Convert 3 2/5 to an improper fraction.

3 × 5 + 2 = 17
Result: 17/5

Example: Convert 23/4 to a mixed number.

23 ÷ 4 = 5 remainder 3
Result: 5 3/4

Comparing Fractions

To compare fractions with different denominators, find a common denominator or cross-multiply.

Example: Which is larger: 3/4 or 5/7?

Method 1 (Common denominator):
LCD of 4 and 7 is 28
3/4 = 21/28
5/7 = 20/28
21/28 > 20/28, so 3/4 > 5/7

Method 2 (Cross-multiply):
3 × 7 = 21
5 × 4 = 20
21 > 20, so 3/4 > 5/7

Equivalent Fractions

Equivalent fractions represent the same value. Create them by multiplying or dividing both numerator and denominator by the same nonzero number.

Example: Find three fractions equivalent to 2/3.

2/3 = 4/6 (multiply by 2/2)
2/3 = 6/9 (multiply by 3/3)
2/3 = 8/12 (multiply by 4/4)

2. Adding and Subtracting Fractions

Same Denominator

When fractions have the same denominator, add or subtract the numerators and keep the denominator.

Examples:
3/7 + 2/7 = 5/7
5/8 - 1/8 = 4/8 = 1/2 (simplified)

Different Denominators

Find the least common denominator (LCD), convert both fractions, then add or subtract.

Example: Calculate 1/4 + 2/3.

LCD of 4 and 3 is 12
1/4 = 3/12
2/3 = 8/12
3/12 + 8/12 = 11/12

Example: Calculate 5/6 - 1/4.

LCD of 6 and 4 is 12
5/6 = 10/12
1/4 = 3/12
10/12 - 3/12 = 7/12

Adding Mixed Numbers

Add the whole numbers separately, then add the fractions. If the fraction sum is improper, convert it.

Example: Add 2 1/3 + 1 2/5.

Whole numbers: 2 + 1 = 3
Fractions: 1/3 + 2/5 = 5/15 + 6/15 = 11/15
Result: 3 11/15

Example with carrying: Add 3 3/4 + 2 5/8.

Whole numbers: 3 + 2 = 5
Fractions: 3/4 + 5/8 = 6/8 + 5/8 = 11/8 = 1 3/8
Result: 5 + 1 3/8 = 6 3/8

Subtracting with Borrowing

When the first fraction is smaller, borrow 1 from the whole number.

Example: Subtract 5 1/4 - 2 3/4.

Since 1/4 < 3/4, borrow 1 from 5
5 1/4 = 4 + 1 + 1/4 = 4 5/4
Now: 4 5/4 - 2 3/4 = 2 2/4 = 2 1/2

3. Multiplying and Dividing Fractions

Multiplying Fractions

Multiply numerators together and denominators together, then simplify.

a/b × c/d = (a × c)/(b × d)

Example: Multiply 2/3 × 4/5.

(2 × 4)/(3 × 5) = 8/15

Example with simplification: Multiply 3/8 × 4/9.

(3 × 4)/(8 × 9) = 12/72 = 1/6

Tip: Cancel before multiplying when possible:
3/8 × 4/9 = (3 × 4)/(8 × 9)
Cancel 3 and 9 (divide by 3): 1/(8) × 4/(3)
Cancel 4 and 8 (divide by 4): 1/(2) × 1/(3) = 1/6

Dividing Fractions

To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal (flip the second fraction).

a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c

Example: Divide 2/3 ÷ 4/5.

2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6

Example: Divide 5/8 ÷ 3.

First write 3 as 3/1
5/8 ÷ 3/1 = 5/8 × 1/3 = 5/24

Multiplying Mixed Numbers

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions first, then multiply.

Example: Multiply 2 1/2 × 1 1/3.

Convert: 2 1/2 = 5/2 and 1 1/3 = 4/3
Multiply: 5/2 × 4/3 = 20/6 = 10/3 = 3 1/3

Complex Fractions

A complex fraction has a fraction in the numerator, denominator, or both. Simplify by treating the entire numerator divided by the entire denominator.

Example: Simplify (2/3)/(4/5).

This means 2/3 ÷ 4/5
= 2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6

Example: Simplify (3 + 1/2)/(2 - 1/4).

Numerator: 3 + 1/2 = 7/2
Denominator: 2 - 1/4 = 7/4
(7/2)/(7/4) = 7/2 × 4/7 = 28/14 = 2

4. Algebraic Fractions

Simplifying Algebraic Fractions

Factor the numerator and denominator, then cancel common factors.

Example: Simplify (6x²)/(9x).

= (6x · x)/(9x)
= (6x)/9 = 2x/3

Example: Simplify (x² - 4)/(x + 2).

Factor numerator: x² - 4 = (x + 2)(x - 2)
= (x + 2)(x - 2)/(x + 2)
= x - 2

Adding Algebraic Fractions

Find a common denominator, just like with numeric fractions.

Example: Add 1/x + 2/(3x).

LCD is 3x
1/x = 3/(3x)
3/(3x) + 2/(3x) = 5/(3x)

Multiplying and Dividing Algebraic Fractions

Use the same rules as numeric fractions.

Example: Multiply (x/4) × (8/x²).

= (x × 8)/(4 × x²)
= 8x/(4x²)
= 2/x

Common Mistakes and Tips

Mistake 1: Adding denominators. When adding fractions, denominators don't add: 1/4 + 1/4 = 2/4, not 2/8.

Mistake 2: Forgetting to flip when dividing. Division requires multiplying by the reciprocal.

Mistake 3: Canceling incorrectly. Can only cancel common factors, not common terms: (x + 2)/2 ≠ x + 1.

Tip 1: Always simplify your final answer.

Tip 2: Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before multiplying or dividing.

Tip 3: Find the LCD systematically. List multiples of each denominator until you find the smallest common one.
