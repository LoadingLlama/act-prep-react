Percentages appear frequently on the ACT Math test. You must know how to calculate simple percentages, handle percent increase and percent decrease, and solve multi-step percentage problems.

1. Simple Percentages

  • Two methods for solving percentage problems:
    • Method 1: Proportion
    • new/original = %/100
    • "Original" is the starting value (100%)
    • "New" is the percentage of the starting value
  • Method 2: Decimal shortcut (FASTER)
    • Convert percentage to decimal by dividing by 100
    • 72% = 0.72, 120% = 1.2, 8.5% = 0.085
    • Multiply: new = original × decimal
    • This is faster and less error-prone!
  • Example: Isaiah bought a watch for 72% of the original $160 price. How much did he pay?
    • Method 1 (Proportion): x/160 = 72/100 → x = $115.20
    • Method 2 (Decimal): 0.72 × 160 = $115.20
    • Both give same answer, but decimal method is faster!
  • Finding the original when given the percentage:
    • Example: Golf clubs cost $720 at 80% of original price. Find original price.
    • Set up: 720 = 0.80 × original
    • Solve: original = 720 ÷ 0.80 = $900

2. Percent Increase

  • The percent increase formula:
    • Percent Increase = (New - Original) ÷ Original × 100
    • Find the difference, divide by original, multiply by 100
    • CRITICAL: Always divide by the ORIGINAL (starting) value
  • Example: Population increases from 500,000 to 620,000. Find percent increase.
    • Step 1: Find the change
    • Change = 620,000 - 500,000 = 120,000
    • Step 2: Divide by original
    • 120,000 ÷ 500,000 = 0.24
    • Step 3: Convert to percentage
    • 0.24 × 100 = 24% increase
  • Shortcut for calculating new value after percent increase:
    • For a 20% increase: multiply by 1.20 (not 0.20!)
    • For a 35% increase: multiply by 1.35
    • For a 150% increase: multiply by 2.50
    • Formula: New = Original × (1 + percent/100)

3. Percent Decrease

  • The percent decrease formula:
    • Percent Decrease = (Original - New) ÷ Original × 100
    • Find the difference, divide by original, multiply by 100
    • Same process as percent increase, just subtract in reverse
  • Example: Price decreases from $80 to $68. Find percent decrease.
    • Step 1: Find the change
    • Change = 80 - 68 = 12
    • Step 2: Divide by original
    • 12 ÷ 80 = 0.15
    • Step 3: Convert to percentage
    • 0.15 × 100 = 15% decrease
  • Shortcut for calculating new value after percent decrease:
    • For a 20% decrease: multiply by 0.80 (which is 1 - 0.20)
    • For a 35% decrease: multiply by 0.65
    • For a 15% decrease: multiply by 0.85
    • Formula: New = Original × (1 - percent/100)

4. Multiple Percentage Changes

  • CRITICAL: Percentage changes are multiplicative, NOT additive
    • A 20% increase followed by a 20% decrease does NOT return to original!
    • Each percentage change applies to the NEW value, not the original
    • Must calculate step-by-step
  • Example: Price starts at $100, increases 20%, then decreases 20%. What's the final price?
    • Step 1: Apply 20% increase
    • $100 × 1.20 = $120
    • Step 2: Apply 20% decrease to NEW value
    • $120 × 0.80 = $96
    • Final price is $96, NOT $100!
  • Shortcut for multiple changes:
    • Multiply all the multipliers together
    • 20% increase then 20% decrease: 1.20 × 0.80 = 0.96
    • Final = Original × 0.96 = $100 × 0.96 = $96
  • Successive increases:
    • Example: 10% increase, then another 10% increase
    • Total: 1.10 × 1.10 = 1.21 (a 21% increase, not 20%!)

5. Common ACT Percentage Scenarios

  • Sales and discounts:
    • 30% off means pay 70% (multiply by 0.70)
    • Sale price = Original × (1 - discount rate)
    • To find original from sale: Original = Sale ÷ (1 - discount rate)
  • Tips and taxes:
    • 15% tip on $50 meal: $50 × 0.15 = $7.50 tip
    • Total with tip: $50 × 1.15 = $57.50
    • Sales tax works the same way
  • Percent of a percent:
    • 20% of 30% of 500 = 0.20 × 0.30 × 500 = 30
    • Multiply all the decimals together
  • What percent is X of Y?
    • Formula: (X ÷ Y) × 100
    • Example: What percent is 15 of 60?
    • (15 ÷ 60) × 100 = 0.25 × 100 = 25%
  • Common mistake to avoid:
    • When finding percent change, ALWAYS divide by the ORIGINAL value
    • Wrong: dividing by the new value
    • Right: (change) ÷ (original) × 100

Key Takeaways

  • ✓Use decimal method for simple percentages (72% of 160 = 0.72 × 160)—faster than proportions!
  • ✓Percent change = (change ÷ original) × 100; ALWAYS divide by original value, not the new value
  • ✓Percent increase: multiply by (1 + rate); percent decrease: multiply by (1 - rate)
  • ✓Multiple percentage changes multiply, not add: 20% up then 20% down = 1.20 × 0.80 = 0.96 (net 4% decrease)
