Ratios and proportions compare quantities and establish relationships between them. On the ACT, you'll use ratios to compare parts, proportions to solve for unknowns, and scale factors to work with similar figures, maps, and real-world applications.

1. Understanding Ratios

Definition and Notation

A ratio compares two quantities. It can be written as a:b, a/b, or "a to b".

  • Part-to-part ratio: Compares one part to another part
  • Part-to-whole ratio: Compares one part to the total
  • Ratios can be simplified like fractions

Example: In a class of 30 students, 18 are girls and 12 are boys.

Girls to boys (part-to-part): 18:12 = 3:2
Girls to total (part-to-whole): 18:30 = 3:5
Boys to total (part-to-whole): 12:30 = 2:5

Working with Ratio Parts

When a ratio is a:b, think of it as "a parts to b parts" of a total of (a + b) parts.

Example: Divide $240 in the ratio 3:5.

Total parts: 3 + 5 = 8
Value of 1 part: 240 ÷ 8 = $30

First amount: 3 × 30 = $90
Second amount: 5 × 30 = $150

Check: 90 + 150 = 240 ✓ and 90:150 = 3:5 ✓

2. Solving Proportions

What is a Proportion?

A proportion states that two ratios are equal: a/b = c/d

Cross-multiplication: If a/b = c/d, then ad = bc

Example: Solve for x: x/12 = 5/8

Cross-multiply:
8x = 12 × 5
8x = 60
x = 7.5

Check: 7.5/12 = 0.625 and 5/8 = 0.625 ✓

Unit Rate Problems

Example: If 5 pounds of apples cost $8, how much do 12 pounds cost?

Set up proportion: 5/8 = 12/x

Cross-multiply:
5x = 8 × 12
5x = 96
x = $19.20

Alternative (unit rate):
Cost per pound: 8 ÷ 5 = $1.60
12 pounds: 12 × 1.60 = $19.20

3. Similar Figures and Scale

Scale Factor

Similar figures have the same shape but different sizes. The scale factor is the ratio of corresponding lengths.

Example: Two similar triangles have corresponding sides of 6 cm and 15 cm. If another side of the smaller triangle is 8 cm, what is the corresponding side of the larger triangle?

Scale factor: 15/6 = 2.5

Corresponding side: 8 × 2.5 = 20 cm

Or use proportion: 6/15 = 8/x
6x = 120
x = 20 cm

Area and Volume Ratios

If the scale factor is k:

  • Lengths are in ratio k:1
  • Areas are in ratio k²:1
  • Volumes are in ratio k³:1

Example: Two similar rectangles have sides in ratio 2:3. If the smaller has area 20 cm², what is the area of the larger?

Scale factor: 3/2 = 1.5
Area factor: (1.5)² = 2.25

Larger area: 20 × 2.25 = 45 cm²

4. Advanced Applications

Three-Way Ratios

Example: Divide 180 in the ratio 2:3:4.

Total parts: 2 + 3 + 4 = 9
One part: 180 ÷ 9 = 20

First amount: 2 × 20 = 40
Second amount: 3 × 20 = 60
Third amount: 4 × 20 = 80

Check: 40 + 60 + 80 = 180 ✓

Map Scales

Example: A map scale is 1:50,000. If two cities are 6 cm apart on the map, what is the actual distance in km?

Map distance: 6 cm
Actual distance: 6 × 50,000 = 300,000 cm

Convert to km: 300,000 cm = 3,000 m = 3 km

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Ratios compare quantities and can be simplified like fractions
  • ✓ Part-to-part ratios compare parts; part-to-whole ratios compare to the total
  • ✓ For ratio a:b, total parts = a + b; find one part value by dividing total by (a + b)
  • ✓ Proportions state two ratios are equal; solve using cross-multiplication
  • ✓ If a/b = c/d, then ad = bc (cross-multiplication rule)
  • ✓ Scale factor k means lengths multiply by k, areas by k², volumes by k³
  • ✓ Unit rate helps solve proportion problems by finding value per one unit
  • ✓ For three-way ratios a:b:c, total parts = a + b + c
  • ✓ Map scales are ratios; multiply map distance by scale factor for actual distance
  • ✓ Always check your answer makes sense in context
