"Which choice" questions ask you to make specific changes to a sentence, and the question itself tells you exactly what to do. These questions are easy to spot—they always include the words "which choice" or "which one" in the question.

1. How to Identify and Approach These Questions

  • Easy to spot: Look for "which choice" or "which one" in the question
    • The question tells you exactly what change to make
    • All answer choices are usually grammatically correct
  • Your job: Figure out what the question asks and do exactly that
    • Don't worry about grammar, conciseness, or style unless the question specifically asks
    • Shorter is NOT always better on these questions
    • Just answer the question—don't overthink it!

2. Type 1: Specific Detail Questions

  • These ask you to provide or emphasize a specific detail
    • Common phrasing: "most specifically describes," "most clearly emphasizes," "provides the most relevant detail"
    • Focus on the sentence you're editing
    • Read sentences before and after for context, but don't rely on them too heavily

Example: Specific Description

  • Question: "Which choice most specifically describes what was used to build iceboxes?"
  • Context: "Americans kept their food cold in iceboxes, which required ice to be bought and placed into the box each week."
    • A. NO CHANGE
    • B. which were only cold enough to keep food cool but not freeze anything
    • C. wooden boxes with tin and insulated with cork and sawdust
    • D. the very first invention that allowed people to keep food cold
  • ✓ Answer: C—it's the only choice that describes the materials used to build iceboxes

3. Type 2: Emphasis and Tone Questions

  • These ask you to emphasize a particular aspect or create a certain tone
    • Common phrasing: "most clearly emphasizes," "best conveys," "creates the most vivid image"
    • Focus on which word choice highlights the specific quality mentioned

Example: Emphasis

  • Question: "Which choice most clearly emphasizes how the dinosaurs increase in size?"
  • Context: "Harold always begged for grow-in-water dinosaurs that sprawl when placed into water."
    • A. NO CHANGE (sprawl)
    • B. change in size
    • C. expand
    • D. soak
  • ✓ Answer: C—"expand" most clearly emphasizes the size increase

4. Common Question Phrasings

  • Questions asking for specific details
    • "Which choice most specifically describes..."
    • "Which choice provides the most relevant detail about..."
    • "Which choice best supports the claim that..."
  • Questions asking for emphasis or tone
    • "Which choice most clearly emphasizes..."
    • "Which choice best conveys..."
    • "Which choice creates the most vivid image of..."
  • The key strategy: Read the question carefully and give it exactly what it asks for—nothing more, nothing less

Hidden Separator

Key Takeaways

  • ✓"Which choice" questions are easy to spot—they always include "which choice" or "which one" in the question. Your job is to figure out exactly what the question asks and select the answer that does that specific thing, not to fix grammar or improve style.
  • ✓All answer choices are usually grammatically correct, so don't worry about grammar unless the question specifically asks about it. Shorter is NOT always better on these questions—choose the answer that best fulfills the specific request.
  • ✓Specific detail questions ask for information about a particular aspect (materials, location, time, etc.). Choose the answer that provides that specific detail, even if other answers are more concise or sound better.
  • ✓Emphasis and tone questions ask you to highlight a particular quality or create a certain feeling. Focus on which word choice best emphasizes the specific aspect mentioned in the question—don't overthink it, just answer what's asked!
