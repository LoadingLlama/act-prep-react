Mastering unit conversion is essential for ACT Math success. These problems test your ability to convert between units of measurement within the same system (like inches to feet) and across systems (like pounds to kilograms). You'll also work with dimensional analysis, conversion factors, and compound units like miles per hour or dollars per square foot. Unit conversion appears in 3-5 questions per ACT—that's roughly 5-8% of all math questions! This lesson covers everything from basic conversions to complex multi-step problems involving rates and mixed units.

1. Basic Unit Conversions

Understanding basic unit conversions forms the foundation for all measurement problems on the ACT. You'll need to memorize common conversion factors and apply them correctly using multiplication or division. The key is knowing whether to multiply or divide based on whether you're converting to larger or smaller units.

Length Conversions (US Customary)

Key conversions to memorize:

  • 1 foot = 12 inches
  • 1 yard = 3 feet = 36 inches
  • 1 mile = 5,280 feet = 1,760 yards

Converting to smaller units: Multiply

Example: Convert 7 feet to inches

7 feet × 12 inches/foot = 84 inches

When converting to smaller units, the number gets larger!

Converting to larger units: Divide

Example: Convert 144 inches to feet

144 inches ÷ 12 inches/foot = 12 feet

When converting to larger units, the number gets smaller!

Length Conversions (Metric System)

Key metric conversions:

  • 1 meter = 100 centimeters = 1,000 millimeters
  • 1 kilometer = 1,000 meters
  • 1 centimeter = 10 millimeters

The metric system uses powers of 10, making conversions straightforward. Move the decimal point right when converting to smaller units, left when converting to larger units.

Examples:

3.5 meters to centimeters:
3.5 m × 100 cm/m = 350 cm

4,200 millimeters to meters:
4,200 mm ÷ 1,000 mm/m = 4.2 m

Weight/Mass Conversions

US Customary:

  • 1 pound = 16 ounces
  • 1 ton = 2,000 pounds

Metric:

  • 1 kilogram = 1,000 grams
  • 1 gram = 1,000 milligrams
  • 1 metric ton = 1,000 kilograms

Volume and Capacity Conversions

US Customary liquid volume:

  • 1 cup = 8 fluid ounces
  • 1 pint = 2 cups = 16 fluid ounces
  • 1 quart = 2 pints = 4 cups = 32 fluid ounces
  • 1 gallon = 4 quarts = 8 pints = 16 cups = 128 fluid ounces

Metric volume:

  • 1 liter = 1,000 milliliters
  • 1 kiloliter = 1,000 liters

Time Conversions

Essential time conversions:

  • 1 minute = 60 seconds
  • 1 hour = 60 minutes = 3,600 seconds
  • 1 day = 24 hours
  • 1 week = 7 days
  • 1 year = 365 days = 52 weeks

Example: Convert 2.5 hours to seconds

Step 1: Convert hours to minutes
2.5 hours × 60 minutes/hour = 150 minutes

Step 2: Convert minutes to seconds
150 minutes × 60 seconds/minute = 9,000 seconds

Or do it in one step:
2.5 hours × 3,600 seconds/hour = 9,000 seconds

ACT Example 1: Basic Length Conversion

A rectangular room measures 15 feet by 18 feet. What is the perimeter of the room in yards?

A) 11 yards

B) 22 yards

C) 33 yards

D) 66 yards

E) 99 yards

Solution:

Step 1: Find the perimeter in feet: P = 2(15) + 2(18) = 30 + 36 = 66 feet

Step 2: Convert feet to yards by dividing by 3 (since 1 yard = 3 feet)

Step 3: 66 feet ÷ 3 feet/yard = 22 yards

Answer: B — Always check what units the answer should be in!

2. Dimensional Analysis

Dimensional analysis, also called the factor-label method, is a systematic approach to unit conversion that uses conversion fractions to cancel unwanted units. This method is particularly powerful for multi-step conversions and prevents common errors. The beauty of dimensional analysis is that units cancel algebraically, just like numbers in a fraction.

Setting Up Conversion Fractions

A conversion fraction is a fraction equal to 1 that relates two different units. For example:

12 inches/1 foot = 1 (both numerator and denominator represent the same length)

1 foot/12 inches = 1 (same conversion, flipped)

60 minutes/1 hour = 1

1,000 meters/1 kilometer = 1

The key is to set up the conversion fraction so that the unit you want to cancel is in the opposite position (numerator vs. denominator) from where it currently appears.

Single-Step Dimensional Analysis

Example: Convert 450 minutes to hours

Set up: 450 minutes × (1 hour / 60 minutes)

Notice: "minutes" appears in the numerator of the starting value and denominator of the conversion fraction, so they cancel

Calculate: 450 × 1/60 = 7.5 hours

Units: minutes × (hours/minutes) = hours ✓

Multi-Step Dimensional Analysis

For conversions requiring multiple steps, chain conversion fractions together. Each fraction cancels one unwanted unit and introduces a new one, creating a "bridge" from starting units to final units.

Example: Convert 3 miles to inches

Plan: miles → feet → inches

Set up:
3 miles × (5,280 feet / 1 mile) × (12 inches / 1 foot)

Cancel: miles cancel, feet cancel, leaving inches

Calculate: 3 × 5,280 × 12 = 190,080 inches

Complex Unit Conversions

When converting units that appear in both numerator and denominator (like speed or density), apply conversion factors to each part separately.

Example: Convert 60 miles/hour to feet/second

Set up:
(60 miles / 1 hour) × (5,280 feet / 1 mile) × (1 hour / 3,600 seconds)

Cancel: miles cancel, hours cancel

Calculate: (60 × 5,280) / 3,600 = 316,800 / 3,600 = 88 feet/second

ACT Example 2: Multi-Step Conversion

A runner completes a 5-kilometer race in 25 minutes. What is the runner's average speed in meters per second?

F) 2.5 meters/second

G) 3.33 meters/second

H) 5 meters/second

J) 8 meters/second

K) 200 meters/second

Solution:

Step 1: Set up the rate: 5 km / 25 min = 0.2 km/min

Step 2: Convert km to meters: 0.2 km/min × 1,000 m/km = 200 m/min

Step 3: Convert minutes to seconds: 200 m/min × 1 min/60 sec = 200/60 = 3.33 m/sec

Answer: G — Work through each conversion systematically to avoid errors.

3. Rate and Compound Unit Conversions

Rate conversions involve units expressed as ratios, such as miles per hour, dollars per pound, or gallons per minute. These compound units require careful attention because you must convert both the numerator and denominator. Understanding rate relationships helps you solve real-world ACT problems involving speed, pricing, and efficiency.

Speed and Distance Rate Conversions

Speed problems on the ACT often require converting between miles per hour (mph), feet per second (ft/s), or meters per second (m/s).

Common speed conversions:

1 mile/hour to feet/second:
1 mph × (5,280 ft / 1 mi) × (1 hr / 3,600 sec) = 1.467 ft/s
Or use the shortcut: multiply mph by 1.467

Reverse (feet/second to miles/hour):
Divide ft/s by 1.467 or multiply by 0.682

Unit Price and Cost Conversions

Price per unit problems require converting between different quantities while maintaining the cost relationship.

Example: Carpet costs $2.50 per square foot. What is the cost per square yard?

Key insight: 1 square yard = 3 ft × 3 ft = 9 square feet

Calculation: $2.50/ft² × 9 ft²/yd² = $22.50/yd²

Logic: Since a square yard contains 9 square feet, it costs 9 times as much!

Area and Volume Conversions

When converting area or volume, remember that you're working with squared or cubed units, so the conversion factors must also be squared or cubed.

Area conversions:

Square inches to square feet:
Since 1 ft = 12 in, then 1 ft² = (12 in)² = 144 in²
Therefore: 1 in² = 1/144 ft²

Square feet to square yards:
Since 1 yd = 3 ft, then 1 yd² = (3 ft)² = 9 ft²
Therefore: 1 ft² = 1/9 yd²

Volume conversions:

Cubic inches to cubic feet:
Since 1 ft = 12 in, then 1 ft³ = (12 in)³ = 1,728 in³

Cubic feet to cubic yards:
Since 1 yd = 3 ft, then 1 yd³ = (3 ft)³ = 27 ft³

Flow Rate Conversions

Flow rates measure volume per time, like gallons per minute or liters per hour.

Example: A faucet flows at 2 gallons per minute. How many quarts per hour is this?

Step 1: Convert gallons to quarts (1 gallon = 4 quarts)
2 gal/min × 4 qt/gal = 8 qt/min

Step 2: Convert minutes to hours (1 hour = 60 minutes)
8 qt/min × 60 min/hr = 480 qt/hr

ACT Example 3: Area Conversion

A rectangular patio measures 12 feet by 15 feet. What is the area of the patio in square yards?

A) 5 square yards

B) 9 square yards

C) 20 square yards

D) 60 square yards

E) 180 square yards

Solution:

Step 1: Find area in square feet: A = 12 × 15 = 180 ft²

Step 2: Remember: 1 yard = 3 feet, so 1 yd² = 9 ft²

Step 3: Convert: 180 ft² × (1 yd² / 9 ft²) = 180/9 = 20 yd²

Answer: C — Don't forget to square the linear conversion factor for area!

4. Cross-System Conversions and Advanced Applications

Cross-system conversions require converting between US Customary and Metric systems, which don't have simple integer relationships. You'll need to memorize key approximate conversion factors and apply them carefully. These problems often appear in real-world contexts involving international measurements, scientific data, or mixed unit systems.

Essential Cross-System Conversions

Length conversions (approximate):

  • 1 inch ≈ 2.54 centimeters (exact)
  • 1 meter ≈ 39.37 inches ≈ 3.28 feet
  • 1 kilometer ≈ 0.621 miles (or 1 mile ≈ 1.61 kilometers)
  • 1 yard ≈ 0.914 meters

Mass/Weight conversions (approximate):

  • 1 kilogram ≈ 2.20 pounds
  • 1 pound ≈ 0.454 kilograms ≈ 454 grams
  • 1 ounce ≈ 28.35 grams

Volume conversions (approximate):

  • 1 liter ≈ 1.06 quarts ≈ 0.264 gallons
  • 1 gallon ≈ 3.785 liters
  • 1 fluid ounce ≈ 29.57 milliliters

Temperature conversions:

Fahrenheit to Celsius:
C = (F - 32) × 5/9

Celsius to Fahrenheit:
F = (C × 9/5) + 32

Key reference points:
Water freezes: 32°F = 0°C
Water boils: 212°F = 100°C
Body temperature: 98.6°F ≈ 37°C
Room temperature: 68°F ≈ 20°C

Multi-Step Cross-System Conversions

Example: Convert 50 miles per hour to meters per second

Plan: miles/hour → kilometers/hour → meters/hour → meters/second

Step 1: Convert miles to kilometers
50 mi/hr × 1.61 km/mi = 80.5 km/hr

Step 2: Convert kilometers to meters
80.5 km/hr × 1,000 m/km = 80,500 m/hr

Step 3: Convert hours to seconds
80,500 m/hr × 1 hr/3,600 sec = 22.36 m/sec

Or in one dimensional analysis chain:
50 mi/hr × 1.61 km/mi × 1,000 m/km × 1 hr/3,600 sec ≈ 22.4 m/sec

Ratio and Proportion in Unit Conversion

Some unit conversion problems are best solved using proportions rather than multiplication. Set up equivalent ratios and cross-multiply to solve.

Example: If 1 inch equals 2.54 centimeters, how many inches is 38.1 centimeters?

Set up proportion:
1 inch / 2.54 cm = x inches / 38.1 cm

Cross-multiply:
2.54x = 38.1

Solve:
x = 38.1 / 2.54 = 15 inches

Currency Exchange as Unit Conversion

Currency exchange problems follow the same principles as unit conversion. Treat different currencies as different units.

Example: If 1 US dollar equals 0.85 euros, and you have 340 euros, how many dollars do you have?

Set up:
340 euros × (1 dollar / 0.85 euros) = 340 / 0.85 = 400 dollars

Or use proportion:
1 dollar / 0.85 euros = x dollars / 340 euros
0.85x = 340
x = 400 dollars

Common Unit Conversion Pitfalls

Mistake 1: Forgetting to square or cube conversion factors for area/volume

Wrong: 100 cm² = 100 ÷ 100 = 1 m² ✗
Right: 100 cm² = 100 ÷ 10,000 = 0.01 m² ✓

Since 1 m = 100 cm, then 1 m² = (100 cm)² = 10,000 cm²

Mistake 2: Multiplying when you should divide (or vice versa)

Use dimensional analysis to avoid this! Set up fractions so units cancel properly.

Converting to smaller units: The number gets larger
Converting to larger units: The number gets smaller

Mistake 3: Using approximate values when exact values are needed

The ACT will specify if you should use an approximation. Otherwise, use exact conversions like:

1 foot = 12 inches (exact)
1 mile = 5,280 feet (exact)
1 hour = 60 minutes (exact)

ACT Example 4: Complex Cross-System Problem

A European recipe calls for 250 grams of flour. If 1 pound equals approximately 454 grams, approximately how many pounds of flour are needed? (Round to the nearest tenth)

F) 0.4 pounds

G) 0.5 pounds

H) 0.6 pounds

J) 1.8 pounds

K) 2.5 pounds

Solution:

Step 1: Set up dimensional analysis: 250 grams × (1 pound / 454 grams)

Step 2: The "grams" unit cancels, leaving pounds

Step 3: Calculate: 250 ÷ 454 ≈ 0.551 pounds

Step 4: Round to nearest tenth: 0.6 pounds

Answer: H — Converting from smaller units (grams) to larger units (pounds) gives a smaller number.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓When converting to smaller units, multiply; when converting to larger units, divide
  • ✓Dimensional analysis ensures units cancel correctly—set up fractions so unwanted units appear in opposite positions
  • ✓For area conversions, square the linear conversion factor (1 ft = 12 in, so 1 ft² = 144 in²)
  • ✓For volume conversions, cube the linear conversion factor (1 ft = 12 in, so 1 ft³ = 1,728 in³)
  • ✓Chain multiple conversion fractions together for multi-step problems, checking that each intermediate unit cancels
  • ✓Rate conversions require converting both numerator and denominator separately
  • ✓Cross-system conversions use approximate factors: 1 mile ≈ 1.61 km, 1 kg ≈ 2.20 lbs, 1 inch = 2.54 cm
  • ✓Temperature conversions use formulas: C = (F - 32) × 5/9 and F = (C × 9/5) + 32
  • ✓Always check what units the answer should be in—the ACT often includes trap answers in wrong units
  • ✓Use exact conversion factors (12, 60, 5280) when given, and approximate factors only when specified
