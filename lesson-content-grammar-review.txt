<p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> This <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">comprehensive grammar review</strong> synthesizes all the major concepts tested on the <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">ACT English Test</strong>. Now that you've studied individual topics, it's essential to develop <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">integrated strategies</strong>, <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">error recognition patterns</strong>, and <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">efficient test-taking approaches</strong>. The ACT doesn't announce which grammar rule each question tests—you must quickly identify error types and apply the appropriate fix. This lesson provides a systematic framework for approaching any grammar question with confidence and speed. </p> <h3 style="margin-top: 5rem; margin-bottom: 0.75rem; font-weight: 700;"> 1. The Complete Grammar Error Checklist </h3> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> When you encounter an underlined portion, systematically check for the most common error types. This mental checklist helps you identify problems quickly without wasting time. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> The "Big 7" Grammar Categories </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> These seven categories account for approximately 70-80% of all grammar questions on the ACT: </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #2563eb; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f8fafc;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>1. Punctuation (15-18 questions)</strong> Commas, semicolons, colons, dashes, apostrophes <strong>2. Sentence Structure (15-18 questions)</strong> Fragments, run-ons, comma splices, subordination <strong>3. Verbs (8-10 questions)</strong> Agreement, tense, form, voice <strong>4. Pronouns (5-7 questions)</strong> </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Agreement, case, clarity, reference <strong>5. Modifiers (4-6 questions)</strong> Misplaced, dangling, squinting modifiers <strong>6. Parallel Structure (3-5 questions)</strong> Lists, conjunctions, comparisons <strong>7. Word Choice &amp; Idioms (3-5 questions)</strong> Commonly confused words, prepositional idioms </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Quick Diagnostic Questions </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Ask yourself these questions in order when you see an underlined portion: </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #16a34a; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f0fdf4;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>→ Is there punctuation?</strong> Check comma rules, <span class="glossary-term" style="color: #2563eb; text-decoration: underline; text-decoration-style: dotted; cursor: help;" title="Punctuation (;) that joins two complete sentences">semicolon</span>/<span class="glossary-term" style="color: #2563eb; text-decoration: underline; text-decoration-style: dotted; cursor: help;" title="Punctuation (:) that introduces a list or explanation">colon</span> usage <strong>→ Is there a verb?</strong> Check subject-verb agreement, tense, form <strong>→ Is there a pronoun?</strong> Check agreement, case, clear antecedent <strong>→ Does the sentence begin with a phrase?</strong> Check for dangling modifiers </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>→ Is there a list or conjunction?</strong> Check parallel structure <strong>→ Can the sentence stand alone?</strong> Check for fragments/run-ons </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Common Error Combinations </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> The ACT often tests multiple concepts in one question. Watch for these combinations: </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #fffbeb;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">Comma + Verb Agreement:</strong> Prepositional phrase between subject and verb, with comma placement <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">Modifier + Pronoun:</strong> Dangling modifier where the pronoun reference is ambiguous <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">Parallel + Verb Tense:</strong> List items with inconsistent verb forms </p> </div> <h3 style="margin-top: 5rem; margin-bottom: 0.75rem; font-weight: 700;"> 2. Systematic Error Recognition Strategies </h3> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Develop a consistent approach to every grammar question. This systematic method prevents you from missing errors and helps you work efficiently under time pressure. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Step 1: Read the Sentence Completely </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Always read from the beginning of the sentence (or previous sentence if needed) through the end. Context matters—you can't identify tense errors, pronoun agreement, or modifier placement without understanding the full sentence. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #2563eb; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f8fafc;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Why this matters:</strong> Many errors depend on elements outside the underlined portion. Subject-verb agreement requires finding the subject (often not underlined). Verb tense requires checking surrounding verbs. Pronoun clarity requires seeing the antecedent. </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Step 2: Trust Your Ear, But Verify with Rules </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> If something sounds wrong, it probably is. However, some errors sound correct (like "everyone...their"), so verify with the rules you've learned. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #16a34a; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f0fdf4;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">Ear + Rules = Accuracy</strong> → If it sounds wrong: Identify which rule is violated → If it sounds right: Still check the Big 7 categories systematically </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Step 3: Eliminate Obviously Wrong Answers </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Use process of elimination to narrow choices before identifying the specific error. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #2563eb; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f8fafc;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Common eliminators:</strong> </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> • Creates a fragment or run-on • Uses wrong verb tense for the context • Creates pronoun agreement error • Breaks parallel structure in a list • Makes the sentence significantly more wordy without adding clarity </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Step 4: Look for Visual Cues </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Certain patterns in the answer choices tell you what the question is testing. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #fffbeb;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Different punctuation marks?</strong> → Punctuation question <strong>Different verb forms?</strong> → Verb agreement or tense <strong>Different pronouns?</strong> → Pronoun agreement or case <strong>Sentence restructured?</strong> → Modifier placement or sentence structure <strong>Different word order in list?</strong> → Parallel structure </p> </div> <h3 style="margin-top: 5rem; margin-bottom: 0.75rem; font-weight: 700;"> 3. Time Management and Pacing </h3> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> The ACT English section gives you 45 minutes for 75 questions across 5 passages. That's 36 seconds per question or 9 minutes per passage. Effective time management is crucial for finishing strong. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> The 9-Minute Per Passage Strategy </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Aim to complete each passage in approximately 9 minutes, which includes reading and answering all 15 questions. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #2563eb; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f8fafc;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Minute 0-2:</strong> Read the passage naturally, answering inline grammar questions as you encounter them <strong>Minute 2-7:</strong> Work through remaining grammar and style questions systematically <strong>Minute 7-9:</strong> Handle rhetorical skills questions (organization, purpose, style) </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Quick vs. Slow Questions </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Not all questions take equal time. Recognize which should be fast and which require more thought. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #16a34a; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f0fdf4;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Quick questions (15-20 seconds):</strong> </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> • Obvious verb agreement errors • Clear punctuation rules (commas with introductory phrases) • Obvious fragments or run-ons • Word choice errors (their/there/they're) </p> </div> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #fffbeb;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Slower questions (45-60 seconds):</strong> </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> • Rhetorical skills (transitions, adding/deleting sentences) • Complex modifier questions requiring sentence restructuring • Pronoun clarity when multiple antecedents possible • Organization questions (sentence placement) </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> When to Skip and Return </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> If you're stuck after 45 seconds, mark your best guess, circle the question number, and move on. Return to it if you have time at the end. </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Critical Strategy:</strong> Never leave questions blank. Even if you're unsure, <span class="glossary-term" style="color: #2563eb; text-decoration: underline; text-decoration-style: dotted; cursor: help;" title="Cross out wrong answer choices">eliminate</span> obviously wrong answers and make an educated guess. There's no guessing penalty on the ACT. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Pacing Checkpoints </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Check your progress at these intervals to ensure you're on track: </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #2563eb; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f8fafc;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">15 minutes:</strong> Should be finishing passage 2 (question 30) <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">30 minutes:</strong> Should be finishing passage 4 (question 60) <strong style="color: #2563eb; font-weight: 600; text-decoration: underline;">40 minutes:</strong> Should be on passage 5 with 5 minutes to finish </p> </div> <h3 style="margin-top: 5rem; margin-bottom: 0.75rem; font-weight: 700;"> 4. Test Day Strategies and Final Tips </h3> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Beyond knowing grammar rules, test day success requires strategic approaches to maximize your score. These final strategies help you navigate the test efficiently and avoid common pitfalls. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> When "NO CHANGE" Is Correct </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> About 20-25% of questions have "NO CHANGE" as the correct answer. Don't avoid this choice if you can't find an error. </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #16a34a; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f0fdf4;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Choose NO CHANGE when:</strong> </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> • You've checked all grammar rules and found no violations • Other answer choices create new errors • Other choices are unnecessarily wordy • The original is clear, concise, and correct </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Shorter Is Usually Better </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> When choosing between grammatically correct options, the ACT typically favors conciseness. If two answers are both correct, choose the shorter one. </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Example:</strong> </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <span style="color: #dc2626;">✗ Wordy:</span> due to the fact that <span style="color: #16a34a;">✓ Concise:</span> because </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>However:</strong> Never sacrifice clarity or correctness for brevity. If the shorter version creates an error or changes meaning, choose the longer correct option. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Avoid Extreme or Absolute Language </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> On rhetorical questions about adding/deleting information or main ideas, be wary of answer choices with extreme words like "always," "never," "only," "completely." </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Read Answer Choices Carefully </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> The ACT loves to include answer choices that look similar but differ in one key detail. Read every word of each choice. </p> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <strong>Common trap:</strong> Three choices are identical except one uses "affects" and another uses "effects." Missing this difference means picking the wrong answer. </p> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Grammar Doesn't Always "Sound" Wrong </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Some grammatically incorrect constructions sound natural in casual speech. Trust the rules over your ear in these cases: </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #f59e0b; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #fffbeb;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> • "Everyone brought their book" sounds fine but is grammatically wrong (everyone = singular) • "Between you and I" sounds formal but is wrong (object of preposition = me) • "The team are winning" sounds right to some but is wrong (team = singular) </p> </div> <h4 style="margin-top: 2rem; margin-bottom: 0.3rem; font-weight: 400;"> Final Mental Checklist </h4> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> Before moving to the next question, quickly verify: </p> <div style="border-left: 4px solid #16a34a; padding: 1rem; margin: 1rem 0; background-color: #f0fdf4;"> <p style="font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.7; margin: 0.5rem 0 1rem 0;"> <span style="color: #16a34a; font-weight: 600;">✓</span> Your answer creates no new errors <span style="color: #16a34a; font-weight: 600;">✓</span> The sentence is complete and logical <span style="color: #16a34a; font-weight: 600;">✓</span> All subjects have verbs and all verbs have subjects <span style="color: #16a34a; font-weight: 600;">✓</span> Pronouns have clear antecedents <span style="color: #16a34a; font-weight: 600;">✓</span> Modifiers are placed next to what they describe </p> </div> <p style="height: 1px; margin: 0; padding: 0;"></p><h3 style="margin-top: 5rem; margin-bottom: 0.75rem; font-weight: 700; visibility: hidden; height: 0; margin: 0; padding: 0; overflow: hidden;">.</h3><h3 style="color: #2e7d32; font-size: 1.4rem; font-weight: 700; margin: 3rem 0 1.5rem 0;"> Key Takeaways </h3> <ul style="list-style: none; padding: 0; margin: 0;"> <li style="margin-bottom: 0.8rem; color: #2e7d32; font-size: 16px; line-height: 1.6;">
